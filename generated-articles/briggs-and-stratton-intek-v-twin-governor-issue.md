## Briggs & Stratton Intek V Twin Governor Issues: A Comprehensive Guide

Have you noticed your Briggs & Stratton Intek V-Twin engine surging, hesitating, or simply not delivering the power it once did? The culprit is often a problem with the governor. This crucial component regulates engine speed, and when it malfunctions, it can lead to frustrating performance issues. This article will dive deep into common Briggs & Stratton Intek V-Twin governor problems, how to diagnose them, and potential solutions to get your engine running smoothly again. We’ll cover everything from simple adjustments to more complex repairs.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular maintenance, including cleaning and lubrication, can prevent governor issues.
*   A surging engine often indicates a governor problem.
*   Checking the governor linkage and spring are crucial first steps in troubleshooting.
*   Replacing worn or damaged parts is often necessary for a lasting repair.

**What Does the Governor Do?**

The governor on your Briggs & Stratton Intek V-Twin engine is responsible for maintaining a consistent engine speed, regardless of the load. It does this by adjusting the throttle based on engine RPM. When the engine tries to slow down under load, the governor opens the throttle to provide more fuel and maintain speed. Conversely, when the engine speeds up, the governor restricts the throttle. A properly functioning governor ensures efficient operation and prevents engine damage.

## Common Symptoms of a Briggs & Stratton Intek V Twin Governor Problem

Identifying the symptoms is the first step toward resolving a governor issue. Several telltale signs indicate a problem with the governor on your Intek V-Twin engine. One of the most common is engine surging – where the engine speed fluctuates up and down erratically. This can be incredibly annoying and make the equipment difficult to use. Another symptom is a lack of power, especially when the engine is under load. You might notice the engine struggles to cut grass, move snow, or perform its intended task. Finally, the engine might stall frequently, particularly when transitioning between different loads. If you experience any of these symptoms, it’s time to investigate the governor.

## Diagnosing the Governor: Step-by-Step

Once you suspect a governor issue, a systematic diagnosis is essential. Start by visually inspecting the governor linkage. Look for any bent, broken, or disconnected parts. The linkage connects the governor arm to the throttle and carburetor. Next, check the governor spring. A weak or broken spring won’t provide the necessary tension to regulate engine speed. You can test the spring by manually moving the governor arm and feeling for resistance. If it feels loose or offers little resistance, the spring likely needs replacing.  Don't forget to check the carburetor; a dirty carburetor can mimic governor issues. You can find helpful guides on [how to clean Briggs and Stratton carburetor](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-briggs-and-stratton-carburetor) to rule this out.

### Checking Governor Arm Movement

The governor arm should move freely and smoothly. If it’s sticking or binding, it could be due to corrosion or lack of lubrication. Apply a small amount of lubricant to the pivot points and try moving the arm again. If it still doesn’t move freely, you may need to disassemble and clean the governor assembly.

## Common Causes of Governor Failure in Intek V-Twin Engines

Several factors can contribute to governor failure in Briggs & Stratton Intek V-Twin engines. One common cause is dirt and debris buildup. Over time, dirt can accumulate in the governor assembly, causing it to stick or bind. Another cause is wear and tear on the governor components, particularly the spring and linkage. Constant use and vibration can eventually lead to fatigue and failure.  Furthermore, improper engine maintenance, such as neglecting oil changes, can accelerate wear and tear on the governor.  Finally, using incorrect fuel or fuel additives can also contribute to governor problems.

## Repairing a Briggs & Stratton Intek V Twin Governor

Repairing a governor issue can range from simple adjustments to complete replacement. If the problem is simply a loose linkage, tightening the connections may be all that’s needed. If the governor spring is weak or broken, replacing it is a relatively straightforward repair. You can find replacement springs at most hardware stores or online retailers. For more complex issues, such as a damaged governor gear or a sticking governor assembly, you may need to disassemble the governor and clean or replace the affected parts. If you're uncomfortable performing these repairs yourself, it’s best to take the engine to a qualified small engine mechanic.

### Replacing the Governor Spring

Replacing the governor spring is a common repair. First, disconnect the governor linkage. Then, remove the old spring and install the new one, ensuring it’s properly seated. Finally, reconnect the governor linkage and test the engine.

## Preventing Future Governor Problems

Preventative maintenance is key to avoiding future governor issues. Regularly clean the engine and governor assembly to remove dirt and debris. Lubricate the governor linkage and pivot points to ensure smooth operation. Use high-quality fuel and oil, and follow the manufacturer’s recommended maintenance schedule.  Proper storage during the off-season can also help prevent corrosion and damage. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can a dirty air filter cause governor problems?**
A: Yes, a dirty air filter restricts airflow to the engine, which can affect engine speed and potentially mimic governor issues. Regularly cleaning or replacing your air filter is crucial for optimal engine performance.

**Q: What if my engine surges even after replacing the governor spring?**
A: If surging persists after replacing the spring, the problem may lie with the governor gear, linkage, or carburetor. Further diagnosis is needed to pinpoint the exact cause.

**Q: Is it difficult to disassemble the governor assembly?**
A: Disassembling the governor assembly can be tricky, as it involves small parts and precise adjustments. If you’re not comfortable with small engine repair, it’s best to consult a professional.

**Q: How often should I lubricate the governor linkage?**
A: Lubricate the governor linkage at least once a year, or more frequently if you use the engine in dusty or dirty conditions.
