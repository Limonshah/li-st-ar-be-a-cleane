## Keeping Your Glide: How To Clean Snowshoes

Have you just finished an exhilarating day trekking through snowy landscapes with your snowshoes? It’s easy to kick them off and forget about maintenance, but proper cleaning is crucial for ensuring your snowshoes last for many winter adventures. Cleaning your snowshoes isn’t difficult, and it protects your investment. This article will walk you through everything you need to know about how to clean snowshoes, from a quick post-trip wipe-down to a more thorough seasonal cleaning.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly remove snow and debris after each use.
*   Wash with mild soap and water for a deeper clean.
*   Dry completely before storing to prevent rust and damage.
*   Inspect bindings and hardware for wear and tear.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean snowshoes, remove excess snow and debris, wash with mild soap and water, rinse thoroughly, and allow them to dry completely before storing. Regular cleaning prevents corrosion and ensures optimal performance for your next snow adventure.

## Why Cleaning Your Snowshoes Matters

Think of your snowshoes as an investment in winter fun. Just like you’d care for your skis or snowboard, snowshoes require attention to stay in good working order. Dirt, salt, and ice can build up, leading to corrosion of metal parts and damage to the bindings. This can affect their performance and even shorten their lifespan. Cleaning your snowshoes regularly will help maintain their functionality and ensure they’re ready for your next outing.

## Post-Trip Snowshoe Maintenance: The Quick Clean

After each use, a quick clean is all you need to keep your snowshoes in good shape. This prevents the build-up of grime that can cause more significant problems later. Here’s how to do it:

*   **Remove Loose Debris:** Use a brush or your hands to remove any snow, ice, or dirt clinging to the snowshoe deck and bindings.
*   **Wipe Down the Frame:** A damp cloth can wipe away any remaining residue from the frame and metal components.
*   **Inspect the Bindings:** Quickly check the bindings for any loose screws or damage. Tighten as needed.
*   **Air Dry:** Allow the snowshoes to air dry completely before storing them.

## Deep Cleaning Your Snowshoes: A Seasonal Approach

At the end of the snowshoeing season, or if your snowshoes are particularly dirty, a more thorough cleaning is necessary. This will remove stubborn grime and prepare them for long-term storage.

*   **Gather Your Supplies:** You’ll need a bucket of warm water, mild dish soap, a soft brush, a garden hose (optional), and clean towels.
*   **Wash the Snowshoes:** Mix the soap with the warm water. Use the brush to scrub the snowshoe deck, frame, and bindings. Pay attention to areas where dirt and grime accumulate.
*   **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the snowshoes with clean water to remove all traces of soap. A garden hose can be helpful for this step, but avoid using high pressure.
*   **Dry Completely:** This is the most important step! Allow the snowshoes to air dry completely in a well-ventilated area. Avoid direct sunlight or heat, which can damage the materials.

## Caring for Different Snowshoe Materials

Snowshoes are made from various materials, each requiring slightly different care. Understanding these differences will help you clean your snowshoes effectively.

*   **Aluminum Frames:** Aluminum is prone to corrosion, so ensure it’s thoroughly dried after washing. You can apply a light coat of silicone spray to help protect it. If you notice any corrosion, consider using a product designed to [clean aluminum](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-aluminum) to restore the metal.
*   **Plastic Decks:** Plastic decks are generally durable and easy to clean. However, avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as they can damage the plastic.
*   **Nylon Bindings:** Nylon bindings can become brittle over time, so avoid exposing them to extreme temperatures or prolonged sunlight.
*   **Steel Crampons:** Steel crampons require special attention to prevent rust. After cleaning, ensure they are completely dry and consider applying a rust inhibitor.

## Binding Maintenance: Ensuring a Secure Fit

The bindings are the most critical part of your snowshoes, ensuring a secure and comfortable fit. Regular maintenance will keep them functioning properly.

*   **Inspect for Wear and Tear:** Check the straps, buckles, and pivots for any signs of damage. Replace any worn or broken parts immediately.
*   **Clean the Buckles:** Dirt and debris can accumulate in the buckles, making them difficult to operate. Use a small brush to clean them thoroughly.
*   **Lubricate Moving Parts:** Apply a small amount of silicone lubricant to the pivots and other moving parts to keep them functioning smoothly.
*   **Check Strap Tension:** Ensure the straps are properly tensioned to provide a secure fit.

## Proper Snowshoe Storage: Protecting Your Investment

How you store your snowshoes during the off-season is just as important as how you clean them. Proper storage will prevent damage and ensure they’re ready to go when the snow flies again.

*   **Clean and Dry:** Before storing, ensure your snowshoes are completely clean and dry.
*   **Loosen Bindings:** Loosen the bindings to relieve tension on the straps and buckles.
*   **Store in a Cool, Dry Place:** Avoid storing snowshoes in direct sunlight, extreme temperatures, or damp environments. A garage, basement, or storage shed is ideal.
*   **Protect from Damage:** Consider storing your snowshoes in a protective bag or covering to prevent scratches and dust accumulation. You might also consider cleaning your [basement](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-basement) before storing your snowshoes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

*   **How often should I clean my snowshoes?**
    After each use, perform a quick clean to remove snow and debris. A deep clean should be done at the end of each season or when they become particularly dirty.
*   **Can I use harsh chemicals to clean my snowshoes?**
    No, avoid using harsh chemicals, abrasive cleaners, or solvents, as they can damage the materials. Mild dish soap and water are the best options.
*   **How can I prevent rust on my snowshoe crampons?**
    Ensure the crampons are completely dry after cleaning and consider applying a rust inhibitor.
*   **What should I do if my snowshoe bindings are broken?**
    Replace any broken or damaged binding parts immediately to ensure a safe and secure fit.
*   **Is it okay to store my snowshoes wet?**
    Absolutely not! Storing wet snowshoes can lead to rust, corrosion, and mold growth. Always ensure they are completely dry before storing.
*   **Can I use a pressure washer to clean my snowshoes?**
    While tempting, avoid using a pressure washer. The high pressure can damage the deck, bindings, and other components.



## Conclusion

Cleaning your snowshoes is a simple yet essential task that will extend their lifespan and ensure optimal performance. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can keep your snowshoes in top condition for many winter adventures to come. Remember to regularly remove snow and debris, wash with mild soap and water, dry completely, and store them properly. Taking care of your snowshoes is an investment in your winter fun, allowing you to continue exploring the beautiful snowy landscapes for years to come. Don’t let a little dirt and grime keep you from enjoying the magic of snowshoeing!
