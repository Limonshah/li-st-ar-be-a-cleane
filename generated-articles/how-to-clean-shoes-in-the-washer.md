## Revitalize Your Kicks: How to Clean Shoes in the Washer

Ever wondered if you could just toss your dirty shoes in the washing machine? The answer is often yes! Cleaning shoes in the washer can be a real time-saver, but it’s not a one-size-fits-all solution. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to safely and effectively wash your shoes, leaving them looking and smelling fresh. We’ll cover which shoes are washer-friendly, how to prepare them, the best washing settings, and how to dry them properly. 

**Takeaway:**

*   Always check the shoe’s care label first.
*   Remove laces and insoles before washing.
*   Use a gentle cycle and cold water.
*   Air dry shoes – avoid the dryer!
*   Protect shoes with a mesh laundry bag.

**Can you really wash shoes in the washing machine?**

Yes, many shoes can be safely cleaned in a washing machine. However, it’s crucial to understand which materials are suitable and to follow the proper steps to avoid damage. Generally, canvas, nylon, and most athletic shoes are good candidates for machine washing.

---

## Which Shoes Can You Wash in the Washer?

Not all shoes are created equal, and some are definitely *not* meant for the washing machine. Knowing which materials can handle the wash cycle is the first step to success. Here's a breakdown:

*   **Safe to Wash:** Canvas shoes, nylon shoes, most sneakers, and synthetic materials generally do well. You can find more specific guidance on [how to clean canvas shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-canvas-shoes) for those specific materials.
*   **Avoid the Washer:** Leather, suede, and delicate materials should *never* go in the washing machine. These materials can be damaged by water and agitation. If you have leather shoes, check out [how to clean leather shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-leather-shoes) for proper care.
*   **Use Caution:** Shoes with embellishments, glued soles, or intricate designs should be washed with extra care or avoided altogether.

---

## Preparing Your Shoes for the Wash

Proper preparation is key to preventing damage and ensuring a thorough clean. Don't skip these steps!

*   **Remove Loose Dirt:** Brush off any excess dirt or debris with a soft brush. A toothbrush works great for getting into crevices.
*   **Remove Laces and Insoles:** Take out the laces and insoles. These can be washed separately by hand or in a mesh laundry bag.
*   **Pre-Treat Stains:** For stubborn stains, apply a small amount of laundry detergent or stain remover directly to the affected area.
*   **Protect Your Washer:** Place your shoes inside a mesh laundry bag or an old pillowcase. This will prevent them from banging around and damaging your washing machine.

---

## Washing Machine Settings: The Right Cycle for Clean Shoes

Choosing the right washing machine settings is crucial for protecting your shoes. Here’s what you need to know:

*   **Water Temperature:** Always use cold water. Hot water can damage adhesives and cause colors to fade.
*   **Cycle:** Select the gentle or delicate cycle. This will minimize agitation and reduce the risk of damage.
*   **Detergent:** Use a small amount of mild laundry detergent. Avoid using bleach, as it can discolor your shoes.
*   **Extra Rinse:** Add an extra rinse cycle to ensure all the detergent is removed. Leftover detergent can leave a residue and attract dirt. If you're concerned about residue, you might also consider cleaning your [how to clean front-load washer](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-front-load-washer) to ensure optimal performance.

---

## Drying Your Shoes: Avoid the Dryer!

This is perhaps the most important step. *Never* put your shoes in the dryer! The heat can warp the shape, melt adhesives, and cause irreversible damage.

*   **Air Dry:** Allow your shoes to air dry naturally at room temperature.
*   **Stuff with Paper:** Stuff the shoes with paper towels or newspaper to help them maintain their shape and absorb excess moisture. Change the paper frequently as it becomes damp.
*   **Avoid Direct Sunlight:** Don't place your shoes in direct sunlight, as this can cause colors to fade.
*   **Drying Time:** Drying time can vary depending on the material and humidity, but it typically takes 24-48 hours.

---

## Dealing with Specific Shoe Issues

Sometimes, your shoes need a little extra attention. Here's how to tackle common problems:

*   **Smelly Shoes:** Add a cup of baking soda to the wash cycle to help neutralize odors. You can also sprinkle baking soda inside the shoes after washing to absorb any lingering smells.
*   **Muddy Shoes:** Rinse off excess mud before washing. You might also consider a pre-soak in lukewarm water with detergent. If you have particularly stubborn mud, you can find tips on [how to clean muddy shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-muddy-shoes).
*   **Scuff Marks:** A magic eraser can often remove scuff marks from rubber soles.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

*   **Q: Can I wash running shoes in the washing machine?**
    A: Yes, most running shoes made of synthetic materials like nylon or mesh can be washed in the washing machine on a gentle cycle with cold water. Always remove the insoles and laces first.
*   **Q: What if my shoes have glued soles?**
    A: Shoes with glued soles are more susceptible to damage. Wash them on the gentlest cycle possible and avoid hot water.
*   **Q: How often can I wash my shoes?**
    A: It depends on how often you wear them and how dirty they get. Washing them every few months or as needed is generally sufficient.
*   **Q: Can I use fabric softener?**
    A: It’s best to avoid fabric softener, as it can leave a residue on your shoes.
*   **Q: What about cleaning the washing machine after washing shoes?**
    A: It's a good idea to run an empty wash cycle with hot water and a cup of vinegar to clean your washing machine after washing shoes. This helps remove any dirt or debris. You can also find more information on [how to clean a dishwasher](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-dish-washer).
