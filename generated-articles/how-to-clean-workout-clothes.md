## How to Clean Workout Clothes: A Complete Guide

Ever wonder why your workout clothes still smell after washing? You’re not alone!  Cleaning workout clothes effectively requires a bit more attention than your everyday laundry. This guide will show you the best way to clean workout clothes, remove stubborn odors, and keep your activewear in top condition. We’ll cover everything from fabric types to washing techniques, ensuring your gear lasts longer and performs better.

**Takeaway:**

*   Wash workout clothes after *every* wear.
*   Turn clothes inside out before washing.
*   Use a gentle, liquid detergent.
*   Avoid fabric softener.
*   Air dry whenever possible.

**Quick Answer:** To effectively clean workout clothes, turn them inside out, wash in cold water with a gentle detergent, and avoid fabric softener. Air drying is best to prevent shrinking and damage to performance fabrics.

## Understanding Your Workout Fabrics

Different workout clothes are made from different materials, and each requires slightly different care. Knowing your fabrics is the first step to keeping them clean and functional.  Let's break down some common types.

*   **Polyester:** This synthetic fabric is popular for its moisture-wicking properties. It’s generally durable but can retain odors.
*   **Spandex (Lycra/Elastane):**  Provides stretch and flexibility. Spandex is sensitive to heat and can lose its shape if not cared for properly.
*   **Nylon:** Another durable synthetic, nylon is often used in leggings and shorts. It’s resistant to abrasion but can also hold onto smells.
*   **Merino Wool:** A natural fiber known for its odor-resistant and temperature-regulating qualities. Merino wool requires gentle handling.
*   **Cotton:** While comfortable, cotton absorbs moisture and takes a long time to dry, making it less ideal for high-intensity workouts.

## Why Workout Clothes Need Special Care

Regular laundry detergents and methods aren't always enough for workout clothes.  These garments are designed to wick away sweat, but that sweat – and the bacteria within it – gets trapped in the fibers. This leads to lingering odors and potential fabric breakdown.  Ignoring this can shorten the lifespan of your favorite activewear.  Plus, built-up residue from detergents can reduce the fabric’s ability to breathe and perform. ## The Best Washing Techniques for Activewear

Now, let’s get into the nitty-gritty of washing.  Following these steps will help you keep your workout clothes fresh and functional.

1.  **Wash After Every Wear:** Don't let sweat sit! Bacteria thrive in damp environments.
2.  **Turn Inside Out:** This protects the outer layer of the fabric and helps flush out sweat and bacteria.
3.  **Cold Water is Key:** Hot water can damage elastic fibers and cause colors to fade.
4.  **Gentle Cycle:**  A delicate cycle minimizes wear and tear on the fabric.
5.  **Liquid Detergent:** Powder detergents can leave residue. Choose a detergent specifically designed for activewear or a gentle, fragrance-free option.
6.  **Skip the Fabric Softener:** Fabric softener coats the fibers, reducing their ability to wick away moisture.
7.  **Avoid Bleach:** Bleach can damage and weaken the fabric.

## Tackling Stubborn Odors

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, odors linger. Here are a few tricks to combat stubborn smells.

*   **Vinegar Soak:** Add 1/2 cup of white vinegar to a basin of cold water and soak your clothes for 30 minutes before washing. Vinegar helps neutralize odors.
*   **Baking Soda Boost:** Add 1/2 cup of baking soda to your washing machine along with your detergent. Baking soda is a natural deodorizer.
*   **Enzyme Detergent:**  These detergents contain enzymes that break down sweat and bacteria.
*   **Pre-Soak with Activewear Wash:**  There are specialized activewear washes available that are designed to remove odors and residue.

## Drying Your Workout Clothes Properly

How you dry your workout clothes is just as important as how you wash them.  Incorrect drying can lead to shrinking, damage, and reduced performance.

*   **Air Dry is Best:**  Hanging your clothes to dry is the gentlest option. It prevents shrinking and preserves the fabric’s elasticity.
*   **Low Heat Tumble Dry (If Necessary):** If you must use a dryer, use the lowest heat setting. High heat can damage synthetic fibers.
*   **Avoid Dryer Sheets:** Similar to fabric softener, dryer sheets can leave a residue that reduces moisture-wicking ability.
*   **Don't Overdry:** Overdrying can cause the fabric to break down.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use bleach on my white workout clothes?**
A: No, bleach can damage the fibers in your workout clothes, especially synthetic materials. It can weaken the fabric and reduce its elasticity. Opt for a vinegar soak or an enzyme detergent instead to brighten whites.

**Q: How often should I wash my workout clothes?**
A: You should wash your workout clothes after *every* wear, even if they don't seem visibly dirty. Sweat and bacteria build up quickly, leading to odors and fabric breakdown.

**Q: What detergent is best for workout clothes?**
A: Choose a gentle, liquid detergent specifically designed for activewear or a fragrance-free option. Avoid detergents with harsh chemicals, bleach, or fabric softener.

**Q: Why do my workout clothes still smell after washing?**
A: Lingering odors are often caused by bacteria trapped in the fabric fibers. Try a vinegar soak, baking soda boost, or enzyme detergent to eliminate the smell.

**Q: Can I iron my workout clothes?**
A: Generally, no. Ironing can damage synthetic fibers like spandex and polyester. If you need to remove wrinkles, use a low-heat setting and a pressing cloth.
