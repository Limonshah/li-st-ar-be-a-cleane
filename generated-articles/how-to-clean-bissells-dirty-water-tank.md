## Keeping Your Bissell Sparkling: How To Clean the Dirty Water Tank

Is your Bissell carpet cleaner losing suction or leaving behind a musty smell? Often, the culprit isn’t the machine itself, but a dirty water tank. A neglected tank can harbor bacteria, mold, and unpleasant odors, impacting your cleaning results and even the air quality in your home. This guide will walk you through a simple, step-by-step process to thoroughly clean your Bissell’s dirty water tank, ensuring optimal performance and hygiene. We’ll cover everything from initial disassembly to effective cleaning solutions and preventative measures.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly empty and rinse the dirty water tank after *every* use.
*   Deep clean the tank with a mild cleaning solution at least once a month.
*   Allow the tank to air dry completely before reassembling.
*   Inspect the tank for cracks or damage and replace if necessary.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean a Bissell dirty water tank, empty it, rinse with warm water, then scrub with a mild detergent or vinegar solution. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely before reattaching to your machine.

## Why Cleaning Your Bissell Dirty Water Tank Matters

Let's face it, the dirty water tank collects *everything* your Bissell pulls from your carpets and floors. This includes dirt, grime, pet dander, and even cleaning solution residue. Over time, this buildup can lead to several problems. A dirty tank can reduce your machine’s suction power, making it less effective at cleaning. It can also create a breeding ground for bacteria and mold, causing unpleasant odors that transfer to your carpets. Regularly cleaning the tank is essential for maintaining your Bissell’s performance and ensuring a hygienic cleaning experience. ## Gathering Your Supplies for a Deep Clean

Before you begin, gather everything you’ll need to make the cleaning process smooth and efficient. You won’t need any specialized tools, just a few common household items. Here’s a checklist:

*   Warm water
*   Mild dish soap or white vinegar
*   Soft-bristled brush (an old toothbrush works great!)
*   Clean cloths or paper towels
*   Rubber gloves (optional, but recommended)
*   Disinfectant spray (optional, for extra sanitation)

Having these supplies ready will save you time and ensure you can complete the cleaning process without interruption. Remember, a little preparation goes a long way!

## Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your Bissell Dirty Water Tank

Now, let's get down to the cleaning process. This is a straightforward procedure that anyone can follow.

1.  **Empty the Tank:**  First, carefully remove the dirty water tank from your Bissell machine. Empty the contents into a toilet or outdoor drain. Avoid pouring dirty water into sinks, as it can clog the drain.
2.  **Initial Rinse:** Rinse the tank thoroughly with warm water. This will remove loose debris and initial grime.
3.  **Prepare Your Cleaning Solution:**  Fill the tank with warm water and add either a tablespoon of mild dish soap or a cup of white vinegar. Vinegar is a natural disinfectant and deodorizer, making it a great alternative to harsh chemicals.
4.  **Scrub-a-Dub-Dub:** Use a soft-bristled brush to scrub the inside of the tank, paying attention to corners and any areas with visible buildup.  Don't use abrasive cleaners or scrub brushes, as these can damage the plastic.
5.  **Rinse Again:**  Empty the soapy or vinegar solution and rinse the tank thoroughly with clean, warm water until all traces of the cleaning solution are gone.
6.  **Disinfect (Optional):** For extra sanitation, spray the inside of the tank with a disinfectant spray and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing again.
7.  **Air Dry:**  Allow the tank to air dry completely before reattaching it to your Bissell machine. This is crucial to prevent mold and mildew growth.

## Dealing with Stubborn Stains and Odors

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, stubborn stains or lingering odors remain. Don't worry, there are a few tricks you can try. For tough stains, create a paste of baking soda and water and apply it to the affected area. Let it sit for 30 minutes before scrubbing and rinsing. If odors persist, fill the tank with a solution of equal parts water and white vinegar and let it soak overnight. ## Preventing Future Buildup and Maintaining Cleanliness

The best way to keep your Bissell’s dirty water tank clean is to prevent buildup in the first place. Here are a few simple habits to adopt:

*   **Empty and Rinse After Every Use:** This is the most important step! Don't let dirty water sit in the tank for extended periods.
*   **Regular Deep Cleaning:**  Deep clean the tank with a mild cleaning solution at least once a month, or more frequently if you use your Bissell often.
*   **Use the Correct Cleaning Solution:**  Always use the cleaning solution recommended by Bissell for your specific machine. Using the wrong solution can cause buildup and damage.
*   **Inspect for Damage:** Regularly inspect the tank for cracks or damage. A damaged tank can leak and harbor bacteria.

## FAQ: Your Burning Questions Answered

**Q: How often should I clean my Bissell dirty water tank?**
A: You should empty and rinse the tank after *every* use. A deep clean with a cleaning solution should be done at least once a month, or more frequently with heavy use, to prevent buildup and odors.

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my Bissell dirty water tank?**
A: While bleach is a powerful disinfectant, it's generally not recommended for cleaning Bissell tanks. Bleach can damage the plastic and leave behind harmful residue.

**Q: My tank still smells after cleaning. What can I do?**
A: Try soaking the tank overnight in a solution of equal parts water and white vinegar. Baking soda can also help absorb odors.

**Q: Is it okay to put the dirty water tank in the dishwasher?**
A: No, it is not recommended to put the dirty water tank in the dishwasher. The high heat can warp or damage the plastic.

**Q: What if my Bissell dirty water tank is cracked?**
A: A cracked tank should be replaced immediately. Continuing to use a damaged tank can lead to leaks and potential damage to your machine.
