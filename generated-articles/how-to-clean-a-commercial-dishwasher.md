## Keeping Your Kitchen Running: How To Clean A Commercial Dishwasher

A clean commercial dishwasher is vital for any food service operation. It’s not just about sparkling dishes; it’s about hygiene, preventing breakdowns, and ensuring your dishwasher lasts. Ignoring regular cleaning can lead to buildup, reduced efficiency, and even costly repairs. This article will guide you through a comprehensive cleaning process, ensuring your dishwasher operates at its best. We’ll cover everything from daily maintenance to deep cleaning schedules, helping you maintain a sanitary and efficient kitchen.

**Takeaway:**

*   Daily rinsing removes food debris.
*   Weekly cleaning targets spray arms and filters.
*   Monthly deep cleaning addresses mineral buildup.
*   Regular maintenance extends dishwasher life.

**Quick Answer:** To clean a commercial dishwasher, start by removing food particles, then clean the spray arms and filters weekly. Monthly, run a dishwasher cleaner to remove mineral buildup and sanitize the machine.

## Why Regular Commercial Dishwasher Cleaning Matters

Let's face it, commercial dishwashers work hard. They tackle piles of dirty dishes all day long, and that constant use leads to buildup. Food particles, grease, and mineral deposits from hard water can accumulate inside, impacting performance. A dirty dishwasher won’t clean dishes effectively, potentially leading to health code violations. Regular cleaning prevents these issues and extends the life of your expensive equipment. It’s a small investment of time that saves you money and headaches in the long run.

## Daily Maintenance: A Quick Rinse is Key

Starting with daily maintenance is the easiest way to keep your commercial dishwasher in good shape. After each shift, quickly rinse down the interior of the dishwasher to remove any large food particles. This prevents these particles from breaking down and causing clogs. Also, check the drain for any visible debris and remove it. This simple step significantly reduces the need for more intensive cleaning later on. Consider this your first line of defense against a dirty dishwasher.

## Weekly Cleaning: Spray Arms, Filters, and More

A weekly cleaning routine is essential for maintaining optimal performance. Begin by turning off and unplugging the dishwasher for safety. Remove the spray arms and soak them in hot, soapy water to dissolve any food residue or mineral deposits. Simultaneously, remove and thoroughly clean the filters. These filters trap food particles, and if they become clogged, the dishwasher won’t function properly. You can find detailed instructions on filter removal in your dishwasher’s manual. For a deeper clean of the interior, wipe down the walls and door with a commercial-grade cleaner. ### Cleaning the Spray Arms Effectively

Spray arms need special attention. Use a small brush or toothpick to clear any blocked nozzles. Clogged nozzles reduce water pressure and cleaning effectiveness. After soaking, rinse the spray arms thoroughly and reattach them securely.

## Monthly Deep Cleaning: Removing Mineral Buildup

Hard water can leave behind mineral deposits inside your commercial dishwasher, reducing its efficiency. Once a month, run a commercial dishwasher cleaner through an empty cycle. These cleaners are specifically formulated to dissolve mineral buildup and sanitize the machine. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully for the best results. This deep cleaning process helps restore your dishwasher’s cleaning power and prevents long-term damage. If you have stubborn buildup, you might need to repeat the process.

## Addressing Specific Issues: Common Problems & Solutions

Sometimes, you’ll encounter specific issues that require targeted solutions. 

*   **Low Water Pressure:** Check the water supply line and ensure it’s fully open. Also, inspect the spray arms for clogs.
*   **Standing Water:** A clogged drain is often the culprit. Check the drain filter and clear any obstructions.
*   **Spotty Dishes:** This could indicate low water temperature or insufficient detergent. Adjust the settings accordingly.
*   **Unpleasant Odors:** Run a cleaning cycle with a dishwasher cleaner and ensure the filters are clean.

## Choosing the Right Cleaning Products

Selecting the right cleaning products is crucial for effective and safe dishwasher maintenance. Always use commercial-grade cleaners specifically designed for dishwashers. Avoid using abrasive cleaners, as they can damage the interior surfaces. Look for cleaners that are formulated to remove grease, food particles, and mineral deposits. Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions for dilution and usage. Remember, using the wrong products can void your warranty and potentially harm your equipment. ## FAQ: Your Commercial Dishwasher Cleaning Questions Answered

**Q: How often should I descale my commercial dishwasher?**
A: Descaling should be done monthly, or more frequently if you have very hard water. Mineral buildup significantly reduces efficiency, so regular descaling is vital.

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my commercial dishwasher?**
A: While bleach can sanitize, it can also damage certain dishwasher components. It’s best to use a commercial dishwasher cleaner specifically designed for the purpose.

**Q: What’s the best way to clean the dishwasher door?**
A: Wipe down the door with a commercial-grade cleaner and a soft cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface.

**Q: How do I know if my dishwasher filter needs replacing?**
A: If the filter is cracked, damaged, or cannot be thoroughly cleaned, it’s time to replace it. A damaged filter won’t effectively trap food particles.

**Q: Is it safe to run a dishwasher cleaning cycle while the dishwasher is in use?**
A: No, always turn off and unplug the dishwasher before performing any cleaning or maintenance. Safety first!
