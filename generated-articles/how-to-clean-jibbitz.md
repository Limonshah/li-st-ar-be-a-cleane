## How To Clean Jibbitz: A Simple Guide

Have you noticed your favorite Jibbitz looking a little dull? These fun Croc charms can quickly accumulate dirt, grime, and even sticky residue. Don't worry, restoring their sparkle is easier than you think! This article will show you exactly [how to clean Jibbitz](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-jibbitz) and keep them looking their best. We’ll cover everything from quick daily wipe-downs to deeper cleaning methods for stubborn buildup. Let's get those charms shining again!

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly wipe Jibbitz with a damp cloth.
*   Use mild soap and water for deeper cleaning.
*   Avoid harsh chemicals that can damage the plastic.
*   Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean Jibbitz, gently remove them from your Crocs. Wash with warm water and a mild soap, using a soft brush for detailed areas. Rinse thoroughly and dry completely before reattaching.

## Why Cleaning Your Jibbitz Matters

Cleaning your Jibbitz isn’t just about aesthetics; it’s about maintaining their quality. Over time, dirt and debris can become embedded in the plastic, making them harder to clean later. Regular cleaning prevents this buildup and keeps your charms looking vibrant. Plus, a clean Jibbitz is a more hygienic Jibbitz, especially if you frequently wear your Crocs in outdoor environments. 

Think about all the places your Crocs take you! From the park to the beach, your Jibbitz are exposed to all sorts of elements. Keeping them clean extends their lifespan and ensures they continue to add personality to your favorite footwear.

## Daily Maintenance: A Quick Wipe-Down

The easiest way to keep your Jibbitz clean is to incorporate a quick wipe-down into your routine. After wearing your Crocs, take a slightly damp cloth and gently wipe each Jibbitz. This removes surface dirt and prevents it from accumulating. It’s a simple step that makes a big difference in the long run. 

This method is perfect for preventing buildup and maintaining a consistent level of cleanliness. You can use a microfiber cloth for best results, as it won’t leave behind lint. If you want to add a little extra cleaning power, you can add a tiny drop of mild dish soap to the damp cloth.

## Deep Cleaning: Soap and Water Method

For Jibbitz that need a more thorough cleaning, the soap and water method is your best bet. Start by gently removing the Jibbitz from your Crocs. Fill a small bowl with warm water and add a few drops of mild dish soap. Let the Jibbitz soak for 5-10 minutes to loosen any dirt or grime.

Next, use a soft-bristled brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub each Jibbitz. Pay attention to any intricate details or areas where dirt is particularly stubborn. Rinse the Jibbitz thoroughly under warm water to remove all traces of soap. Finally, dry them completely with a clean cloth before reattaching them to your Crocs. You can also let them air dry.

## Tackling Stubborn Residue

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, Jibbitz can develop stubborn residue. This could be from sticky substances like juice, candy, or even sunscreen. For these situations, a little bit of rubbing alcohol can be helpful. 

Dampen a cotton swab with rubbing alcohol and gently rub it over the affected area. Be careful not to use too much alcohol, as it can potentially damage the plastic. Once the residue is removed, rinse the Jibbitz thoroughly with water and dry completely. If you're concerned about the alcohol, test it on an inconspicuous area of the Jibbitz first. While you want to get your Jibbitz clean, it’s important to avoid using harsh materials or chemicals. Abrasive cleaners, like scouring pads or harsh chemicals, can scratch or damage the plastic. Avoid using bleach, ammonia, or any solvents, as these can discolor or melt the Jibbitz.

## Storage Tips to Keep Jibbitz Clean

Proper storage can help prevent your Jibbitz from getting dirty in the first place. When you’re not using them, store your Jibbitz in a clean, dry container. A small plastic bag or a dedicated storage box works well. This protects them from dust, dirt, and accidental damage. 

Avoid storing them in direct sunlight, as this can cause the colors to fade over time. If you have a large collection, consider organizing them by color or theme for easy access. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q: Can I put my Jibbitz in the dishwasher?**

A: No, it’s not recommended to put Jibbitz in the dishwasher. The high heat and harsh detergents can damage the plastic and cause them to warp or melt. Hand washing is the safest and most effective method.

**Q: How do I clean Jibbitz with glitter?**

A: Glitter Jibbitz require extra care. Gently hand wash them with mild soap and water, avoiding excessive scrubbing that could remove the glitter.

**Q: Will rubbing alcohol damage my Jibbitz?**

A: Rubbing alcohol can be used sparingly to remove stubborn residue, but it’s important to test it on an inconspicuous area first. Use it in moderation and rinse thoroughly afterward.

**Q: How often should I clean my Jibbitz?**

A: It depends on how often you wear your Crocs and the environments you expose them to. A quick wipe-down after each wear is ideal, with a deeper cleaning every few weeks or as needed.

**Q: What if my Jibbitz are still dirty after cleaning?**

A: If your Jibbitz are still dirty, try soaking them for a longer period of time or using a slightly stronger solution of mild soap and water. You can also try using a specialized plastic cleaner, but always test it in an inconspicuous area first.
