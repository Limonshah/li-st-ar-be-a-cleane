Consistent, gentle care is the best way to keep your Pura Vida bracelets clean.  A little prevention goes a long way! ## Deep Cleaning: A Step-by-Step Guide

Sometimes, a simple wipe-down isn’t enough.  For bracelets that are visibly dirty or have accumulated grime, a deeper cleaning is necessary. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

1.  **Prepare a Cleaning Solution:** Mix a small amount of mild liquid soap (like dish soap or hand soap) with lukewarm water. Avoid using harsh detergents or bleach, as these can damage the string and fade the colors.
2.  **Gently Wash:** Dip a soft cloth or toothbrush into the cleaning solution and gently scrub the bracelet. Pay attention to areas with visible dirt or grime.
3.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the bracelet under lukewarm running water, ensuring all soap residue is removed.
4.  **Dry Carefully:** Gently pat the bracelet dry with a clean towel.  Avoid wringing or twisting the bracelet, as this can damage the string. Lay the bracelet flat to air dry completely.

## What to Avoid When Cleaning Your Pura Vida Bracelet

Certain cleaning methods can damage your Pura Vida bracelet.  It’s crucial to know what *not* to do.  Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach, ammonia, or acetone. These can discolor the string and damage any metal components.  Don’t soak the bracelet for extended periods, as this can weaken the string.  Never put your bracelet in the washing machine or dryer.  Also, avoid using abrasive cleaners or scrubbing brushes, as these can scratch the beads or damage the string.  Remember, gentle is key!

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q: Can I use hand sanitizer to clean my Pura Vida bracelet?**
A: While hand sanitizer can kill germs, it contains alcohol which can dry out and damage the string material. It’s best to stick to mild soap and water.

**Q: How often should I clean my Pura Vida bracelet?**
A: It depends on how often you wear it and the conditions it’s exposed to. A quick wipe-down after each wear is ideal, and a deeper cleaning every few weeks or as needed.

**Q: My bracelet smells bad. What should I do?**
A: Soak the bracelet in a solution of mild soap and water for a short period, then rinse thoroughly and air dry. Baking soda can also help neutralize odors.

**Q: Can I put my Pura Vida bracelet in the dishwasher?**
A: Absolutely not! The high heat and harsh detergents in a dishwasher will likely damage the string and any beads or charms.

**Q: What's the best way to store my Pura Vida bracelets?**
A: Store them in a jewelry box or pouch to protect them from dust, scratches, and sunlight.
