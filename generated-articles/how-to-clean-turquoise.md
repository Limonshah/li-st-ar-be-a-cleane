## How to Clean Turquoise: A Simple Guide

Have you ever wondered how to keep your beautiful turquoise jewelry looking its best? Turquoise is a stunning gemstone, but its porous nature makes it a little more delicate than other stones. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about cleaning turquoise, ensuring it retains its vibrant color and natural beauty for years to come. We’ll cover safe cleaning methods, what to avoid, and how to prevent future damage. Let's dive in and learn how to properly care for your treasured turquoise pieces!

**Takeaway:**

*   Avoid harsh chemicals and abrasive cleaners.
*   Use a soft cloth and mild soap for gentle cleaning.
*   Dry thoroughly after cleaning to prevent water spots.
*   Store turquoise separately to avoid scratches.

**Quick Answer:** To clean turquoise, gently wipe it with a soft, damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid harsh chemicals, ultrasonic cleaners, and prolonged water exposure. Dry immediately with a soft cloth to maintain its color and prevent damage.

## Understanding Turquoise and Its Sensitivity

Turquoise is a hydrated phosphate of copper and aluminum, which means it contains water within its structure. This is what gives turquoise its beautiful color, but it also makes it relatively soft and porous. Because of this porosity, turquoise easily absorbs oils, dirt, and even cleaning solutions.  It’s important to understand this sensitivity when choosing a cleaning method.  Different types of turquoise also exist, including stabilized and treated varieties, which may have slightly different care requirements. 

## Gentle Cleaning: The Best Approach for Turquoise

The safest and most effective way to clean turquoise is with a gentle approach.  You’ll need a few simple supplies: a soft cloth (microfiber is ideal), lukewarm water, and a mild soap – think baby shampoo or a gentle dish soap.  First, dampen the cloth with water and add a tiny drop of soap. Gently wipe the turquoise, paying attention to any areas with visible dirt or grime.  Avoid scrubbing, as this can damage the stone’s surface.  

## Removing Dirt and Oils from Turquoise Jewelry

Oils from your skin and everyday wear can dull the brilliance of your turquoise.  To remove these, follow the gentle cleaning method described above. For more stubborn grime, you can let the soapy water sit on the turquoise for a very short time – no more than a minute.  Then, gently wipe away the dirt.  If your turquoise is set in silver, you can also clean the silver using a [silver cleaning guide](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-silver) but be careful to avoid getting the silver cleaner on the turquoise itself.

### Cleaning Stabilized Turquoise

Stabilized turquoise has been treated with a resin to make it harder and less porous. This means it's a bit more durable than natural turquoise, but it still requires gentle cleaning. You can use the same mild soap and water method, but you might be able to gently scrub a little more if needed.  However, always err on the side of caution.

## What to Avoid When Cleaning Turquoise

Certain cleaning methods can severely damage turquoise.  Here’s a list of things to *never* do:

*   **Harsh Chemicals:** Avoid bleach, ammonia, and other strong cleaning agents.
*   **Ultrasonic Cleaners:** The vibrations can damage the stone.
*   **Steam Cleaners:**  The heat and moisture can be detrimental.
*   **Abrasive Cleaners:**  Scouring powders or abrasive cloths will scratch the surface.
*   **Prolonged Water Exposure:**  Don't soak turquoise for extended periods.

## Drying and Polishing Your Turquoise

After cleaning, it’s crucial to dry your turquoise thoroughly. Use a clean, soft cloth to gently pat the stone dry.  Don't rub vigorously, as this could cause scratches.  Once dry, you can lightly polish the turquoise with a clean, dry microfiber cloth to restore its shine.  Proper drying prevents water spots and helps maintain the stone’s color.  If you're concerned about restoring a dull finish, consider consulting a professional jeweler.

## Storing Turquoise to Prevent Tarnishing and Damage

Proper storage is just as important as cleaning.  Turquoise should be stored separately from other jewelry to prevent scratches.  Wrap each piece in a soft cloth or place it in a separate jewelry box compartment.  Avoid storing turquoise in direct sunlight or in humid environments, as this can affect its color and stability.  Keeping your turquoise stored correctly will help preserve its beauty for years to come.  You might also consider storing it with a small silica gel packet to absorb any excess moisture.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Turquoise

**Q: Can I use baking soda to clean turquoise?**
A: No, baking soda is mildly abrasive and can scratch the surface of turquoise. It's best to stick to mild soap and water.

**Q: How often should I clean my turquoise jewelry?**
A:  Clean your turquoise jewelry whenever it looks dirty or dull.  Generally, a gentle cleaning every few weeks is sufficient, depending on how often you wear it.

**Q: What if my turquoise has a coating?**
A: Some turquoise is coated with a wax or resin to enhance its color and protect it. If your turquoise is coated, be extra gentle when cleaning and avoid harsh chemicals.

**Q: Can I clean turquoise with a jewelry cleaning machine?**
A: No, jewelry cleaning machines, especially ultrasonic cleaners, are not recommended for turquoise. The vibrations can cause damage.

**Q: My turquoise looks faded. Can I restore its color?**
A:  Fading can occur over time, especially with natural turquoise. While you can't fully restore the original color, gentle cleaning and proper storage can help prevent further fading. A professional jeweler might be able to offer further treatment options.
