## Keeping Your Pool Flowing: How To Clean Out Pool Lines

Is your pool water circulation sluggish? Do you notice reduced suction from your skimmer or cleaner? The culprit could be clogged pool lines. Cleaning out these lines is crucial for maintaining a healthy and efficient pool. This article will guide you through the process of cleaning your pool lines, ensuring optimal water flow and a beautifully clean pool. We’ll cover everything from identifying blockages to effective cleaning methods, so you can enjoy a worry-free swimming season.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly check for reduced water flow.
*   Backwashing your filter is a first step to address minor clogs.
*   A pool line blowing system is the most effective cleaning method.
*   Preventative maintenance can minimize future blockages.

**What Does It Mean to Clean Out Pool Lines?**

Cleaning out pool lines refers to removing debris – leaves, dirt, algae, and even scale – that accumulates inside the pipes connecting your pool equipment. This buildup restricts water flow, impacting your filter’s efficiency and overall pool sanitation. A clear understanding of this process is essential for any pool owner wanting to maintain a pristine swimming environment.

---

## Understanding Your Pool’s Plumbing System

Before diving into cleaning, it’s helpful to understand your pool’s plumbing. Your pool has two main types of lines: suction side and return side. Suction lines pull water *from* the pool, through the skimmer and main drain, to the pump and filter. Return lines push filtered water *back* into the pool through the jets. Knowing the difference helps you pinpoint where a blockage might be occurring.  Understanding this system will help you troubleshoot issues and maintain optimal performance. ### Identifying Common Blockage Locations

Certain areas are more prone to clogs. The skimmer line often accumulates leaves and debris. The main drain line can collect dirt and algae, especially if the pool isn’t covered.  Lines leading to and from the heater or salt chlorine generator can develop scale buildup. Regularly inspecting these areas can help you catch problems early.

## The First Step: Backwashing Your Filter

Often, reduced flow isn’t due to a major blockage, but simply a dirty filter. Backwashing reverses the water flow through the filter, flushing out trapped debris. This is a quick and easy first step before tackling the lines directly. Consult your filter’s manual for specific backwashing instructions.  Backwashing is a simple way to improve water flow and extend the life of your filter. ## The Most Effective Method: Using a Pool Line Blowing System

The most effective way to clean out pool lines is with a pool line blowing system. This involves using a powerful air compressor to force air through the lines, dislodging debris. 

### What You’ll Need:

*   **Air Compressor:**  A compressor capable of delivering at least 30 PSI is recommended.
*   **Pool Line Blowing Adapter:** This connects your compressor to the skimmer or return lines.
*   **Safety Glasses:** Protect your eyes from flying debris.
*   **Gloves:** Protect your hands.

### The Process:

1.  **Turn off the pump and filter.** This is crucial for safety.
2.  **Connect the adapter to the skimmer or return line.**
3.  **Connect the air compressor to the adapter.**
4.  **Slowly increase the air pressure to 30 PSI.** *Never exceed the recommended pressure.*
5.  **Blow air through the lines in short bursts.** You’ll likely see debris exiting through the return jets or skimmer.
6.  **Repeat the process for each line.**  Work systematically to ensure all lines are cleared.

## Alternative Cleaning Methods (For Minor Clogs)

While a blowing system is best, you can try these for minor blockages.

### Using a Garden Hose

A garden hose with a high-pressure nozzle can sometimes dislodge debris. Carefully insert the hose into the skimmer or return line and direct the water flow to push out the blockage. Be cautious not to use excessive pressure, which could damage the lines.

### Chemical Line Cleaners

There are chemical line cleaners available specifically for pools. These dissolve organic matter like algae and biofilm. Follow the product instructions carefully, as some chemicals can be harsh on pool equipment.  Always prioritize safety when using pool chemicals.

## Preventing Future Clogs: Proactive Maintenance

Prevention is always better than cure. Here are some tips to minimize future blockages:

*   **Regularly skim the pool surface.** Removing leaves and debris before they sink prevents them from entering the lines.
*   **Keep your pool covered when not in use.** A cover prevents debris from falling into the pool.
*   **Maintain proper water chemistry.** Balanced water chemistry prevents scale buildup.
*   **Flush lines annually.** Even if you don’t notice any issues, an annual line flush can prevent problems.
*   **Consider a leaf catcher.** Installing a leaf catcher in your skimmer basket can prevent large debris from entering the lines. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I clean my pool lines?**
A: Ideally, you should thoroughly clean your pool lines annually, even if you don't notice any issues. Regular preventative maintenance can save you from more significant problems down the road.

**Q: Can I use a wet/dry vacuum to clean my pool lines?**
A: While a wet/dry vacuum *might* work for very minor clogs, it’s not as effective as a pool line blowing system and could potentially damage the lines.

**Q: What if I can’t get the blockage out with the blowing system?**
A: If the blockage persists, you may need to call a professional pool service technician. They have specialized equipment and expertise to handle more stubborn clogs.

**Q: Is it safe to use high pressure when blowing out the lines?**
A: No. Never exceed the recommended pressure of 30 PSI. Excessive pressure can damage the pool lines and equipment.
