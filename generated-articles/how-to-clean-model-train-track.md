## Keeping Your Railway Running: How To Clean Model Train Track

Is your model train sputtering, stopping unexpectedly, or just not performing as well as it used to? The culprit is often dirty track. A clean track ensures reliable electrical contact, allowing your locomotives and rolling stock to operate smoothly. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about how to clean model train track, from simple daily maintenance to more thorough cleaning methods. We’ll cover different techniques and tools to get your railway back on track – literally!

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents performance issues.
*   Use appropriate cleaning methods for your track type.
*   Preventative measures minimize future cleaning needs.
*   Proper cleaning extends the life of your trains and track.

**Quick Answer:** To clean model train track, use a track cleaning car, a dedicated track cleaning fluid, or a clean cloth with isopropyl alcohol. Gently scrub the rails to remove oxidation and debris, ensuring good electrical contact for smooth train operation.

## Why Model Train Track Gets Dirty

Model train track accumulates dirt and grime surprisingly quickly. Dust, oxidation, and debris from the environment all contribute to a loss of conductivity. Over time, this buildup interferes with the electrical current needed to power your trains. You might notice flickering lights, intermittent movement, or complete stalls. Understanding *why* your track gets dirty helps you develop a consistent cleaning routine. 

Here's a breakdown of common contaminants:

*   **Oxidation:** Metal reacts with air, forming a layer of corrosion.
*   **Dust & Debris:** Everyday household dust settles on the rails.
*   **Oil & Fingerprints:** Handling track can leave behind oils.
*   **Track Lubricant:** While necessary, lubricant can attract dust.

## Cleaning Methods: From Simple to Intensive

Now, let's explore different cleaning methods, starting with the easiest and progressing to more intensive techniques. The best method depends on the level of dirt and the type of track you have.

### 1. Daily/Regular Maintenance with a Track Cleaning Car

A track cleaning car is your first line of defense. Run it regularly, even if your track doesn’t *look* dirty. This prevents buildup and keeps your trains running smoothly. Simply attach the car to your locomotive and run it around your layout. The cleaning pad will scrub the rails, removing light dust and oxidation. This is a quick and easy way to maintain your track.

### 2. Using Track Cleaning Fluid

For more stubborn dirt, use a dedicated track cleaning fluid. Apply the fluid to a clean cloth and gently wipe the rails. Avoid pouring the fluid directly onto the track, as this can damage scenery or plastic components. Let the fluid sit for a few minutes to dissolve the grime, then wipe away the residue with a clean cloth. ### 3. Isopropyl Alcohol Cleaning

Isopropyl alcohol is a powerful solvent that can remove tough oxidation and residue. Dampen a clean cloth with isopropyl alcohol (91% or higher) and carefully wipe the rails. Be cautious when using alcohol, as it can damage some plastics. Test it on an inconspicuous area first. Ensure the alcohol evaporates completely before running trains.

### 4. Rail Erasers for Heavy Oxidation

If your track has significant oxidation, a rail eraser can be helpful. Gently rub the eraser along the rails to remove the corrosion. Be careful not to apply too much pressure, as this can damage the track. Rail erasers are abrasive, so use them sparingly.

## Preventing Track From Getting Dirty

Prevention is better than cure! Here are some tips to minimize track cleaning:

*   **Cover Your Layout:** When not in use, cover your layout to protect it from dust.
*   **Air Filtration:** Use an air purifier in the room to reduce airborne dust.
*   **Avoid Touching the Rails:** Handle track as little as possible to prevent oil and fingerprint contamination.
*   **Proper Storage:** Store track in a clean, dry place when not in use.
*   **Regular Inspections:** Regularly inspect your track for dirt and debris.



## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I clean my model train track?**

A: It depends on usage and environment. Generally, run a track cleaning car weekly and perform a more thorough cleaning with fluid or alcohol every 1-3 months.

**Q: Can I use water to clean my model train track?**

A: No, water can cause corrosion and damage electrical components. Always use dedicated track cleaning fluids or isopropyl alcohol.

**Q: What's the best way to clean a rusty track?**

A: Use a rail eraser to gently remove the rust. Follow up with isopropyl alcohol to remove any remaining residue.

**Q: Will track cleaning fluid damage my scenery?**

A: Yes, some fluids can. Apply fluid to a cloth instead of directly to the track, and avoid getting it on scenery.

**Q: Is there a difference between cleaning HO and Lionel track?**

A: Yes, Lionel track often requires more aggressive cleaning due to its larger size and tendency to oxidize.
