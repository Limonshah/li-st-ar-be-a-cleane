## Craftsman Riding Mower Forward/Reverse Issues: A Troubleshooting Guide

Have you ever been halfway through mowing your lawn only to find your Craftsman riding mower stubbornly refusing to go forward? It’s a frustrating situation, especially when it works perfectly fine in reverse. This is a surprisingly common problem with these mowers, and thankfully, it’s often something you can diagnose and fix yourself. This article will walk you through the most likely causes of why your Craftsman riding mower will go in reverse but not forward, and provide step-by-step instructions to get you back on track. We’ll cover everything from simple fixes to more involved repairs.

**Takeaway:**

*   Check the drive belt for wear or breakage.
*   Inspect the transmission fluid level and condition.
*   Examine the forward/reverse linkage for damage or disconnection.
*   Consider a clogged or faulty hydrostatic filter.



**Quick Answer:**

A Craftsman riding mower going in reverse but not forward usually indicates a problem with the transmission, drive belt, or the linkage connecting the shift lever to the transmission. Often, a worn or broken drive belt is the culprit, but issues with the transmission fluid or internal transmission components are also common.

## 1. Understanding Your Craftsman Mower’s Drive System

Before diving into repairs, it’s helpful to understand how your Craftsman riding mower moves. Most Craftsman riding mowers utilize either a belt-driven system or a hydrostatic transmission. Belt-driven mowers use a series of belts to transfer power from the engine to the wheels. Hydrostatic transmissions use hydraulic fluid to provide smooth, variable speed control. Knowing which system your mower has will help narrow down the potential causes of the problem. If you're unsure, consult your owner's manual. A well-maintained mower is a happy mower, and regular checks can prevent these issues.

## 2. Inspecting the Drive Belt: A Common Culprit

The drive belt is often the first place to start when your mower won’t go forward. This belt is responsible for transferring power to the wheels, and if it’s worn, cracked, or broken, it can prevent forward motion while still allowing reverse to function. To inspect the belt, you’ll typically need to remove the mower deck. Carefully examine the entire length of the belt for any signs of damage. A frayed or broken belt needs to be replaced. Replacing a drive belt is a relatively straightforward repair, and replacement belts are readily available. You can find helpful guides online, and even watch videos demonstrating the process. ## 3. Checking the Transmission Fluid Level and Condition

If the drive belt appears to be in good condition, the problem might lie within the transmission itself. Low or contaminated transmission fluid can cause the mower to lose forward drive. Check the transmission fluid level using the dipstick, following the instructions in your owner’s manual. The fluid should be at the appropriate level and appear clean and red. If the fluid is low, top it off with the recommended type of transmission fluid. If the fluid is dark, murky, or smells burnt, it needs to be drained and replaced. Regularly changing your transmission fluid is crucial for maintaining the longevity of your mower.

## 4. Examining the Forward/Reverse Linkage

The linkage connects the shift lever to the transmission, allowing you to select forward, reverse, or neutral. If this linkage is damaged, disconnected, or misadjusted, it can prevent the mower from engaging forward gear. Inspect the linkage for any broken or bent components. Ensure all connections are secure and that the linkage moves freely. Sometimes, a simple adjustment of the linkage can resolve the issue. If you find any damaged parts, they will need to be replaced.

## 5. Hydrostatic Transmission Filter Issues (Hydrostatic Models)

If your Craftsman mower has a hydrostatic transmission, a clogged or faulty hydrostatic filter can restrict fluid flow and prevent forward movement. The filter is designed to keep the hydraulic fluid clean, but over time, it can become clogged with debris. Locate the hydrostatic filter (refer to your owner’s manual) and inspect it for blockage. Replace the filter if it appears dirty or clogged. Maintaining a clean filter is essential for optimal hydrostatic transmission performance.

## 6. Investigating Internal Transmission Problems

If you’ve checked the belt, fluid, linkage, and filter (if applicable) and your mower still won’t go forward, the problem might be internal to the transmission. Internal transmission problems can be more complex and may require professional repair. Common internal issues include worn gears, damaged hydraulic components, or a faulty hydrostatic pump. Diagnosing these issues often requires specialized tools and knowledge. If you’re not comfortable working with internal transmission components, it’s best to take your mower to a qualified repair shop. ## 7. Carburetor and Engine Considerations

While less common, a poorly running engine can sometimes *seem* like a transmission issue. If the engine isn't producing enough power, it might struggle to engage the forward gear, especially on inclines. A dirty carburetor can restrict fuel flow and reduce engine power. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Why does my Craftsman mower work in reverse but not forward?**
A: This usually indicates a problem with the transmission, drive belt, or the linkage connecting the shift lever to the transmission. A worn or broken drive belt is a common cause, but issues with transmission fluid or internal transmission components are also possible.

**Q: How often should I change the transmission fluid in my Craftsman riding mower?**
A:  Generally, you should change the transmission fluid every 25-50 hours of operation, or at least once a year. Refer to your owner’s manual for specific recommendations.

**Q: Can I use any type of transmission fluid in my Craftsman mower?**
A: No, it’s crucial to use the type of transmission fluid recommended in your owner’s manual. Using the wrong fluid can damage the transmission.

**Q: Is it difficult to replace the drive belt on a Craftsman riding mower?**
A: Replacing the drive belt is a relatively straightforward repair for someone with basic mechanical skills. There are many online resources and videos available to guide you through the process.

**Q: What if I’ve tried all these fixes and my mower still won’t go forward?**
A: If you’ve exhausted all the troubleshooting steps and your mower still won’t go forward, it’s likely there’s an internal transmission problem that requires professional repair.



## Conclusion

Dealing with a Craftsman riding mower that goes in reverse but not forward can be frustrating, but it’s often a fixable problem. By systematically checking the drive belt, transmission fluid, linkage, and filter (if applicable), you can often identify and resolve the issue yourself. Remember to always consult your owner’s manual for specific instructions and safety precautions. Don't hesitate to seek professional help if you're uncomfortable with any of the repairs. Getting your Craftsman mower back in forward gear will have you enjoying a beautifully mowed lawn in no time!
