## Understanding Moldavite and Its Cleaning Needs

Moldavite is a unique tektite formed from a meteorite impact in what is now the Czech Republic. These imperfections are part of its beauty and should be respected during cleaning.

## Gentle Physical Cleaning of Moldavite

The first step in cleaning Moldavite is removing any physical dirt or debris. This should always be done with care to avoid scratching or damaging the stone. Here’s how to do it:

*   **Lukewarm Water:** Fill a bowl with lukewarm water. Avoid hot or cold water, as temperature shocks can potentially harm the stone.
*   **Soft Cloth:** Dip a soft, lint-free cloth into the water and gently wipe the Moldavite. A microfiber cloth works exceptionally well.
*   **Mild Soap (Optional):** If necessary, you can add a tiny drop of mild, pH-neutral soap to the water. Ensure all soap residue is thoroughly rinsed away.
*   **Avoid Harsh Chemicals:** Never use harsh chemicals, abrasive cleaners, or solvents like alcohol or bleach on Moldavite. These can damage the surface and alter its color.
*   **Drying:** Pat the Moldavite dry with a clean, soft cloth.  Allow it to air dry completely before handling or storing it. ## What to Avoid When Cleaning Moldavite

Knowing what *not* to do is just as important as knowing what to do. Here’s a list of things to avoid when cleaning Moldavite:

*   **Ultrasonic Cleaners:** These can damage the stone’s structure.
*   **Steam Cleaners:** The high heat can cause cracking.
*   **Harsh Chemicals:** Avoid bleach, ammonia, and other strong cleaning agents.
*   **Abrasive Materials:** Do not use scouring pads, steel wool, or abrasive powders.
*   **Prolonged Water Exposure:** While a gentle wash is fine, avoid soaking Moldavite for extended periods.
*   **Extreme Temperatures:** Avoid sudden temperature changes.
