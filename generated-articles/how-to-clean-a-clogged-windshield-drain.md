## Keeping Your View Clear: How To Clean A Clogged Windshield Drain

Have you noticed water leaking inside your car, particularly near the windshield? It’s likely a clogged windshield drain. These small drains are designed to channel water away from your car’s body, preventing rust and keeping your interior dry. Ignoring a clog can lead to bigger problems, like mold growth and electrical issues. This article will show you how to effectively clean a clogged windshield drain, restoring proper water flow and protecting your vehicle. We’ll cover everything from identifying the drains to the best tools and techniques for clearing them.

**Takeaway:**

*   Locate the windshield drain holes.
*   Use compressed air, thin wire, or a small tool to dislodge debris.
*   Flush the drains with water to confirm they are clear.
*   Regularly inspect and clean drains to prevent future clogs.

**What Exactly *Is* a Windshield Drain and Why Do They Clog?**

Windshield drains are small channels, usually located at the corners of your windshield where it meets the hood. They’re essential for directing rainwater and wash fluid away from the car’s interior. Over time, these drains can become clogged with debris like leaves, dirt, pollen, and even insect nests. This blockage prevents water from escaping, leading to leaks inside the car, potentially damaging electrical components and fostering mold growth. Addressing this issue promptly is crucial for maintaining your vehicle’s health.

## Identifying Your Windshield Drain Locations

Before you start cleaning, you need to find the drains. Typically, you’ll find two drains at the base of the windshield, one on each side. They are often hidden under the plastic trim.  Look for small slots or holes. Sometimes, you might need to gently lift the edge of the trim to get a clear view. Knowing exactly where these drains are located will make the cleaning process much easier.  If you're having trouble locating them, consult your car’s owner’s manual for specific instructions.

## Tools You’ll Need to Clear the Blockage

Gathering the right tools will make this job a breeze. You don’t need anything fancy! Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

*   **Compressed Air:** This is often the most effective method.
*   **Thin Wire or Stiff Bristle Brush:** A paperclip, guitar string, or small detailing brush can work.
*   **Water:** A small spray bottle or a gentle stream from a hose.
*   **Gloves (Optional):** To keep your hands clean.
*   **Small Flashlight:** Helpful for seeing into the drain holes.

Having these tools on hand will ensure you can tackle the clog efficiently.  You can find compressed air canisters at most auto parts stores.

## Using Compressed Air to Blast Away Debris

Compressed air is a fantastic way to dislodge clogs without damaging anything. Hold the nozzle of the compressed air can close to the drain opening and give short, controlled bursts of air.  Avoid holding the can upside down, as this can release liquid propellant. You should hear a puff of air and potentially see debris being expelled. Repeat this process several times, alternating between the two drains.  This method is often enough to clear minor blockages.  For more stubborn clogs, move on to the next step. ## The Wire or Bristle Brush Technique for Stubborn Clogs

If compressed air doesn’t do the trick, it’s time to get a little more hands-on. Carefully insert a thin wire or a small stiff-bristle brush into the drain opening. Gently probe around to break up the clog. Avoid using excessive force, as you could damage the drain tube.  Rotate the wire or brush as you insert it to help dislodge debris. Once you feel resistance, gently work to break up the blockage.  This method is particularly effective for removing leaves, twigs, and other solid materials.  Remember to be patient and persistent.

## Flushing the Drains to Confirm Clear Passage

After using compressed air or a wire, it’s essential to flush the drains with water to ensure they’re completely clear. Use a small spray bottle filled with water or a gentle stream from a hose.  Pour or direct the water into the drain opening and observe if it flows freely out from under the car, usually near the firewall. If the water backs up, the drain is still clogged. Repeat the previous steps until the water flows freely. This step confirms that the drain is open and functioning correctly.  Proper drainage is vital for preventing future water damage.

## Preventing Future Clogs: Regular Maintenance Tips

Preventing clogs is much easier than dealing with them after they happen. Here are a few tips to keep your windshield drains clear:

*   **Regular Inspection:** Check the drains every few months, especially after heavy rain or leaf fall.
*   **Leaf Blower:** Use a leaf blower to clear debris from around the base of the windshield.
*   **Gentle Flushing:** Periodically flush the drains with water as a preventative measure.
*   **Park Smart:** Avoid parking under trees that shed a lot of leaves.

Taking these simple steps can save you time, money, and the hassle of dealing with a clogged windshield drain. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I clean my windshield drains?**

A: Ideally, you should inspect and clean your windshield drains at least twice a year, in the spring and fall. However, if you live in an area with a lot of trees or experience frequent heavy rain, you may need to clean them more often.

**Q: What if I can’t get the clog out myself?**

A: If you’ve tried the methods above and still can’t clear the clog, it’s best to take your car to a professional mechanic. They have specialized tools and expertise to handle more stubborn blockages.

**Q: Is it normal for a little water to drip after cleaning the drains?**

A: Yes, it’s normal for a small amount of water to drip out after cleaning the drains. This is simply residual water that was trapped in the tubes. It should stop within a few minutes.

**Q: Can a clogged windshield drain cause mold?**

A: Absolutely. If water is trapped inside your car due to a clogged drain, it can create a damp environment that promotes mold growth. Mold can be harmful to your health and cause unpleasant odors.

**Q: What tools should I *avoid* using to clean the drains?**

A: Avoid using anything sharp or abrasive that could damage the drain tubes. Don't use large tools that won't fit inside the drain opening.
