## Revitalize Your Clean: How to Clean a Shark Navigator Brush Roll

Is your Shark Navigator vacuum losing suction? A clogged brush roll is often the culprit!  Keeping your brush roll clean is essential for maintaining optimal performance and ensuring a truly deep clean of your floors. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about how to clean a Shark Navigator brush roll, step-by-step. We’ll cover removal, cleaning methods, and preventative measures to keep your vacuum running smoothly.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly remove hair and debris from the brush roll.
*   Use scissors or a seam ripper to cut tangled fibers.
*   Clean the brush roll with gentle soap and water.
*   Ensure the brush roll is completely dry before reinstalling.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean a Shark Navigator brush roll, first disconnect the vacuum. Then, remove the brush roll cover and the brush roll itself. Cut away any tangled hair or string, and wash the brush roll with mild soap and water. Finally, let it dry completely before reinstalling.



## 1. Why Cleaning Your Shark Navigator Brush Roll Matters

A dirty brush roll significantly impacts your vacuum’s ability to pick up dirt, dust, and debris. Hair, string, and fibers quickly wrap around the brush, reducing its effectiveness and potentially damaging the motor.  This leads to decreased suction and a less thorough clean.  Regular cleaning prevents these issues, extending the life of your vacuum and keeping your home cleaner.  Think of it like this: a clean brush roll means a happier, more efficient vacuum!

## 2. Gathering Your Supplies for Brush Roll Cleaning

Before you begin, gather everything you’ll need to make the process smooth and efficient. You won’t need any special tools, just a few common household items. This preparation will save you time and frustration. Here’s a checklist:

*   **Small Scissors or Seam Ripper:** For cutting tangled hair and fibers.
*   **Screwdriver (if needed):** Some models require a screwdriver to remove the brush roll cover.
*   **Warm Water:** For washing the brush roll.
*   **Mild Dish Soap:** To help loosen dirt and grime.
*   **Old Towel:** For drying the brush roll.
*   **Cleaning Brush (optional):** A small brush can help remove stubborn debris.



## 3. Removing the Brush Roll from Your Shark Navigator

The process for removing the brush roll varies slightly depending on your specific Shark Navigator model. However, the general steps are similar.  Always refer to your vacuum’s manual for specific instructions.  Generally, you’ll need to access the brush roll compartment, usually located at the bottom of the vacuum head. 

*   **Disconnect the Vacuum:** Safety first! Always unplug your vacuum before performing any maintenance.
*   **Access the Brush Roll Compartment:**  Look for a release button or latch on the bottom of the vacuum head.
*   **Remove the Brush Roll Cover:** Once the latch is released, the cover should lift off.
*   **Remove the Brush Roll:** Gently pull the brush roll out of the compartment.  If it’s stuck, try rotating it slightly. ## 4.  Tackling Tangled Hair and Debris

This is often the most time-consuming part of the process, but it’s crucial for restoring your brush roll’s performance.  Hair, string, and fibers can wrap tightly around the brush, forming a stubborn mess.  Don’t pull or yank, as this can damage the bristles.

*   **Use Scissors or a Seam Ripper:** Carefully cut through the tangled fibers. Work slowly and methodically to avoid damaging the brush roll.
*   **Pull Away Loose Debris:**  Remove any loose hair or debris by hand.
*   **Inspect for Hidden Tangled Areas:**  Check between the bristles for any remaining tangled material.
*   **Consider a Cleaning Tool:** A small brush can help dislodge stubborn debris.



## 5. Washing and Drying the Brush Roll

Once you’ve removed all the tangled debris, it’s time to wash the brush roll. This will remove any remaining dirt and grime.  Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the bristles.

*   **Prepare a Warm Water and Soap Solution:** Fill a sink or basin with warm water and a small amount of mild dish soap.
*   **Wash the Brush Roll:** Submerge the brush roll in the soapy water and gently scrub it with your hands or a soft brush.
*   **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the brush roll under clean, running water until all the soap is removed.
*   **Dry Completely:** Lay the brush roll on an old towel and allow it to air dry completely. *This is very important!*  Do not reinstall the brush roll until it is completely dry to prevent mold and mildew growth.  If you need to speed up the drying process, you can gently wipe it down with a clean towel.



## 6.  Preventative Maintenance for a Cleaner Brush Roll

Regular cleaning is key, but you can also take steps to prevent excessive buildup on your brush roll.  These simple habits will save you time and effort in the long run.

*   **Vacuum Regularly:** Frequent vacuuming prevents dirt and debris from accumulating.
*   **Pre-Clean Rugs and Carpets:** Remove large debris from rugs and carpets before vacuuming.
*   **Avoid Vacuuming Loose Items:** Pick up small toys, cords, and other loose items before vacuuming.
*   **Check the Brush Roll Regularly:**  Inspect the brush roll for tangled debris at least once a month. ## 7. Reinstalling the Brush Roll and Testing

Once the brush roll is completely dry, it’s time to reinstall it.  This is a simple process, but make sure everything is properly aligned.

*   **Reinsert the Brush Roll:** Carefully slide the brush roll back into the brush roll compartment.
*   **Replace the Brush Roll Cover:**  Align the cover and snap it back into place.
*   **Test the Vacuum:** Plug in the vacuum and test it on a small area of carpet or flooring.  Listen for any unusual noises and ensure the brush roll is spinning freely. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I clean my Shark Navigator brush roll?**
A:  It depends on how often you vacuum and the type of flooring you have.  Generally, cleaning the brush roll every 1-3 months is a good practice. If you have pets or long hair, you may need to clean it more frequently.

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my brush roll?**
A: No, avoid using bleach or harsh chemicals, as they can damage the bristles and potentially harm your vacuum. Mild dish soap and water are sufficient.

**Q: My brush roll is still tangled after cutting. What can I do?**
A: Try using a seam ripper to carefully pick at the tangled fibers.  Patience is key!  You may also need to soak the brush roll in warm, soapy water for a few minutes to loosen the debris.

**Q:  What if my brush roll isn’t spinning after cleaning?**
A:  Ensure the brush roll is completely dry and properly seated in the compartment.  Check for any obstructions preventing it from spinning. If the problem persists, consult your vacuum’s manual or contact Shark customer support.
