## Keeping Your Swimsuit Sparkling: How to Clean Bathing Suits

Summer is synonymous with sunshine, beaches, and, of course, bathing suits! But after a day of fun in the sun and surf, your swimsuit needs some serious attention. Knowing how to clean bathing suits properly isn’t just about removing sand and chlorine; it’s about preserving the fabric, color, and shape so you can enjoy it for seasons to come. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to keep your swimwear looking its best. We’ll cover everything from hand washing to machine washing, dealing with stubborn stains, and proper drying techniques.

**Takeaway:**

*   Always rinse your swimsuit immediately after use.
*   Hand washing is the gentlest method for cleaning bathing suits.
*   Use a mild detergent specifically designed for delicates.
*   Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach and fabric softener.
*   Air dry your swimsuit away from direct sunlight.

**Quick Answer:** To clean a bathing suit, rinse it immediately after swimming, hand wash with mild detergent in cool water, gently squeeze out excess water (don't wring!), and air dry flat away from direct sunlight.

## Why Proper Bathing Suit Cleaning Matters

Did you know that chlorine, saltwater, and even sunscreen can damage your swimsuit over time? These elements break down the elastic fibers, causing your suit to lose its shape and become faded. Regular and proper cleaning helps to counteract these effects, extending the life of your favorite swimwear. Ignoring proper care can lead to stretched-out suits, discoloration, and a generally worn-out appearance.  Investing a little time in cleaning will save you money in the long run by preventing premature replacement.

## Hand Washing: The Gentle Approach

Hand washing is the most recommended method for cleaning bathing suits, especially those made from delicate materials like Lycra or Spandex. It’s a gentle process that minimizes stretching and fading. Here’s how to do it:

1.  **Rinse Immediately:** As soon as you’re done swimming, rinse your swimsuit thoroughly with cool, clean water. This removes chlorine, saltwater, and sand before they can set in.
2.  **Prepare the Wash:** Fill a clean sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of mild detergent – look for detergents specifically designed for delicates or swimwear. [https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-a-backpack](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-a-backpack) offers great advice on choosing the right cleaning products for delicate fabrics.
3.  **Wash Gently:** Submerge your swimsuit and gently swirl it around in the water for a few minutes. Avoid harsh scrubbing or twisting.
4.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the swimsuit several times with cool, clean water until all traces of detergent are gone.
5.  **Remove Excess Water:** Gently squeeze out excess water – *never* wring or twist the suit, as this can damage the fibers. You can also gently roll it in a clean towel to absorb some of the moisture.

## Machine Washing: When It's Okay (and How to Do It)

While hand washing is preferred, machine washing is sometimes necessary. If you choose to machine wash, take these precautions:

*   **Use a Laundry Bag:** Place your swimsuit inside a mesh laundry bag to protect it from snagging or getting tangled with other clothes.
*   **Gentle Cycle:** Select the delicate or gentle cycle on your washing machine.
*   **Cool Water:** Always use cool water. Hot water can damage the elastic and cause fading.
*   **Mild Detergent:** Use a mild detergent, as mentioned above. Avoid bleach and fabric softener. Fabric softener can coat the fibers and reduce the suit’s elasticity.
*   **Wash with Similar Colors:** Wash your swimsuit with other similar colors to prevent dye transfer.

## Tackling Stubborn Stains on Your Swimsuit

Sometimes, rinsing isn’t enough to remove all the grime. Here’s how to deal with common swimsuit stains:

*   **Sunscreen Stains:** Pretreat the stain with a small amount of dish soap and gently rub it in. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes before washing.
*   **Chlorine Stains:** Soak the swimsuit in a solution of one part white vinegar to three parts cool water for about 30 minutes. Then, wash as usual.
*   **Oil or Lotion Stains:** Sprinkle baking soda on the stain and let it sit for several hours to absorb the oil. Brush off the baking soda and wash as usual.
*   **Sand:** Before washing, shake out as much sand as possible.  If sand remains, consider rinsing the suit inside out to dislodge it. ## The Right Way to Dry Your Bathing Suit

Proper drying is just as important as proper washing. Here’s what to avoid:

*   **Direct Sunlight:** Avoid drying your swimsuit in direct sunlight, as this can cause the colors to fade and the elastic to break down.
*   **Dryer:** *Never* put your swimsuit in the dryer. The heat will damage the fabric and cause it to shrink.
*   **Hanging:** Hanging a wet swimsuit can stretch it out of shape.

Instead, lay your swimsuit flat on a clean, dry towel or hang it on a plastic hanger away from direct sunlight. Ensure it’s fully dry before storing it.

## Storing Your Swimsuit for the Off-Season

Once your swimsuit is completely dry, store it properly to keep it in good condition for next summer. Avoid folding it tightly, as this can create creases and damage the elastic. Instead, roll it loosely or lay it flat in a drawer.  Storing it in a breathable bag will also help prevent mildew. ## FAQ About Cleaning Bathing Suits

**Q: Can I use bleach on my white bathing suit?**

A: No, avoid using bleach on your bathing suit, even if it’s white. Bleach can weaken the fabric and cause it to deteriorate quickly. Opt for a mild detergent and a longer soak time if needed.

**Q: How often should I clean my bathing suit?**

A: You should rinse your swimsuit after *every* use and wash it after *every 1-2 wears*, or whenever it starts to smell or feel dirty.

**Q: What if my bathing suit has a strong chlorine smell?**

A: Soak the swimsuit in a solution of one part white vinegar to three parts cool water for at least 30 minutes before washing.

**Q: Is it okay to wash my bathing suit with my regular laundry?**

A: It’s not recommended. The agitation and harsh detergents in regular laundry can damage your swimsuit. Always wash it separately on a delicate cycle with mild detergent.

**Q: Can I iron my bathing suit?**

A: No, do not iron your bathing suit. The heat will melt the elastic and damage the fabric.

Remember, a little preventative care goes a long way in preserving your investment and ensuring you’re ready for your next beach adventure.
