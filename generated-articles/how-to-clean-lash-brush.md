## Keeping Those Lashes Happy: How To Clean Your Lash Brush

Ever wonder about the cleanliness of the little tool you use so close to your eyes? A dirty lash brush can harbor bacteria, leading to irritation or even infection. Cleaning your lash brush regularly is a simple step that makes a huge difference in your eye health and the performance of your mascara. This article will guide you through everything you need to know about how to clean a lash brush, ensuring you get the most out of your favorite lash-enhancing products. We’ll cover different cleaning methods, how often to clean, and why it’s so important.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly cleaning your lash brush prevents bacteria buildup.
*   Gentle cleansers like baby shampoo or lash cleanser work best.
*   Proper drying is crucial to avoid mold growth.
*   Cleaning extends the life of your lash brush and mascara.

**Quick Answer:** To clean a lash brush, gently wash it with baby shampoo or a lash cleanser in lukewarm water. Rinse thoroughly and reshape the bristles. Allow it to air dry completely, bristles facing up, before reuse.

## Why Cleaning Your Lash Brush Matters

Think about everything your lash brush encounters – mascara, eye makeup, oils from your skin, and even potential bacteria. Over time, this buildup can harden, making your mascara clumpy and less effective. More importantly, it creates a breeding ground for bacteria. Using a contaminated lash brush can introduce these bacteria to your eyes, potentially causing irritation, styes, or even conjunctivitis. Cleaning your lash brush isn’t just about aesthetics; it’s about protecting your eye health. It’s a small habit with a big impact.

## The Best Cleansers for Your Lash Brush

Choosing the right cleanser is key to effectively cleaning your lash brush without damaging it. Harsh chemicals can break down the bristles and affect their performance. Here are some excellent options:

*   **Baby Shampoo:** This is a gentle and readily available option. Its mild formula effectively removes mascara and debris without being too harsh.
*   **Lash Cleanser:** Specifically formulated for lash extensions and tools, these cleansers are designed to be gentle yet effective.
*   **Micellar Water:** A popular makeup remover, micellar water can also be used to clean your lash brush, though it may require a bit more rinsing.
*   **Diluted Gentle Soap:** If you don’t have baby shampoo or lash cleanser, a very diluted solution of gentle, fragrance-free soap can work in a pinch.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your Lash Brush

Now that you know *why* and *what* to use, let’s get to the *how*. Here’s a simple, step-by-step guide to cleaning your lash brush:

1.  **Rinse with Lukewarm Water:** Start by rinsing the brush under lukewarm water to remove any loose mascara.
2.  **Apply Cleanser:** Place a small amount of your chosen cleanser (baby shampoo, lash cleanser, etc.) into the palm of your hand.
3.  **Gently Swirl:** Gently swirl the brush in the cleanser, using circular motions to work the product into the bristles. Avoid getting cleanser into the metal ferrule (the part that connects the bristles to the handle).
4.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the brush thoroughly under lukewarm water until all traces of cleanser are gone.
5.  **Reshape Bristles:** Gently reshape the bristles with your fingers to restore their original form.
6.  **Air Dry:** Place the brush on a clean towel, bristles facing up, and allow it to air dry completely. *Never* use a hairdryer, as the heat can damage the bristles.

## How Often Should You Clean Your Lash Brush?

The frequency of cleaning depends on how often you use your mascara. However, a good rule of thumb is to clean your lash brush at least **once a week**. If you use mascara daily, consider cleaning it 2-3 times a week. Additionally, always clean your lash brush after it comes into contact with any eye infection or irritation. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and keeps your brush hygienic. ## Extending the Life of Your Lash Brush & Mascara

Cleaning your lash brush isn’t just about hygiene; it also helps extend the life of both the brush and your mascara. By removing buildup, you prevent the mascara from drying out and becoming clumpy. This means you can use your mascara for longer, saving you money in the long run. Additionally, a clean brush applies mascara more smoothly and evenly, giving you better results. Remember to replace your mascara every 3 months, even if you clean your brush regularly, as the formula itself can degrade over time.

## Drying Your Lash Brush: Avoiding Bacteria Growth

Proper drying is just as important as cleaning. A damp brush is a breeding ground for bacteria and mold. Always ensure your lash brush is completely dry before storing it. Here are a few tips:

*   **Air Dry Only:** Avoid using heat to dry the brush, as this can damage the bristles.
*   **Bristles Up:** Place the brush on a clean towel with the bristles facing up to allow for optimal airflow.
*   **Avoid Enclosed Spaces:** Don’t store the brush in a closed container until it’s completely dry.
*   **Check for Moisture:** Before using the brush, double-check that the bristles are completely dry to the touch.

## FAQ About Cleaning Lash Brushes

**Q: Can I use makeup remover to clean my lash brush?**

A: While you *can* use micellar water, it's not the most effective option. Baby shampoo or a lash cleanser are better at removing mascara buildup.

**Q: What if my lash brush is really old and caked with mascara?**

A: Soak the brush in warm water with a bit of baby shampoo for 10-15 minutes before attempting to clean it. This will help loosen the buildup.

**Q: Can I clean a disposable mascara wand?**

A: Yes, you can! Cleaning disposable wands extends their usability and reduces waste. However, remember they are not designed for long-term use.

**Q: Is it okay to share a lash brush?**

A: Absolutely not! Sharing a lash brush can spread bacteria and infections. Each person should have their own brush.
