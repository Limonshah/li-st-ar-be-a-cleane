## How To Clean A Dermal Piercing: A Complete Guide

Have you just gotten a dermal piercing and are wondering about the best way to keep it clean? Proper cleaning is crucial for successful healing and preventing infection. A dermal piercing, unlike traditional piercings, sits *under* the skin, making aftercare a little different. This article will walk you through everything you need to know about how to clean a dermal piercing, from the initial cleaning to recognizing potential problems. We’ll cover the best solutions, techniques, and what to watch out for to ensure your new piercing heals beautifully.

**Takeaway:**

*   Clean your dermal piercing twice daily with sterile saline solution.
*   Avoid harsh chemicals, alcohol, and touching the piercing with dirty hands.
*   Watch for signs of infection and seek professional help if needed.

**Quick Answer:** To clean a dermal piercing, use a sterile saline solution twice a day. Gently spray the piercing and surrounding skin, then pat dry with a clean, disposable paper towel. Avoid harsh soaps, alcohol, and picking at the piercing to promote healthy healing.

## Understanding Dermal Piercings & Why Cleaning Matters

Dermal piercings are created by inserting jewelry into a pocket made beneath the skin. This differs from traditional piercings that go *through* a fold of skin. Because of this unique placement, they require specific aftercare to avoid complications. Cleaning removes bacteria, debris, and bodily fluids that can lead to infection. A clean piercing heals faster and looks better. Ignoring proper cleaning can result in painful infections, scarring, and even the loss of the piercing. 

## What You’ll Need to Clean Your Dermal Piercing

Before you start, gather your supplies. Using the right tools is just as important as the cleaning process itself. Here’s what you’ll need:

*   **Sterile Saline Solution:** This is the gold standard for cleaning piercings. Look for a solution specifically labeled as sterile, containing 0.9% sodium chloride. Avoid solutions with additives.
*   **Disposable Paper Towels:** These are preferable to cloth towels, as they won’t harbor bacteria.
*   **Clean Hands:** Always wash your hands thoroughly with antibacterial soap and water before touching your piercing.
*   **Optional: Gloves:** If you prefer, you can wear disposable gloves for extra hygiene.

## The Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your Dermal Piercing

Now, let's get to the cleaning process. Consistency is key, so aim to do this twice a day. 

1.  **Wash Your Hands:** Begin by thoroughly washing your hands with antibacterial soap and warm water. This prevents introducing new bacteria to the piercing.
2.  **Spray with Saline:** Hold the sterile saline solution a few inches away from your piercing. Gently spray the piercing and the surrounding skin. Don’t saturate the area, just apply enough to moisten it.
3.  **Gently Pat Dry:** Use a clean, disposable paper towel to gently pat the area dry. Avoid rubbing, as this can irritate the piercing.
4.  **Inspect for Issues:** While cleaning, take a moment to inspect the piercing for any signs of infection (more on that later).

## What *Not* To Use When Cleaning Your Dermal Piercing

Just as important as knowing what *to* use is knowing what *not* to use. Certain products can hinder healing and even cause serious problems. 

*   **Alcohol:** Alcohol is too harsh and can dry out the skin, delaying healing.
*   **Hydrogen Peroxide:** Similar to alcohol, hydrogen peroxide can be damaging to the delicate skin around the piercing.
*   **Antibacterial Soaps:** While antibacterial soap is good for your hands, it's often too harsh for a fresh piercing.
*   **Ointments:** Ointments can trap bacteria and prevent air circulation, increasing the risk of infection.
*   **Harsh Chemicals:** Avoid perfumes, lotions, and other chemicals near the piercing.

## Recognizing and Addressing Signs of Infection

Even with diligent cleaning, infections can sometimes occur. Knowing the signs of infection is crucial for prompt treatment. Look out for:

*   **Excessive Redness:** Some redness is normal immediately after piercing, but increasing redness is a concern.
*   **Swelling:** Significant swelling around the piercing site.
*   **Pain:** Increased or worsening pain.
*   **Discharge:** Yellow or green discharge (clear fluid is normal initially).
*   **Fever:** A fever can indicate a systemic infection.

If you suspect an infection, **do not** remove the jewelry. Removing the jewelry can trap the infection inside. Instead, consult a piercer or medical professional immediately. They can assess the situation and recommend appropriate treatment, which may include antibiotics. ## Long-Term Care & Maintaining a Healthy Dermal Piercing

Once your dermal piercing has healed (which can take several months), you can reduce the frequency of cleaning to once a day or as needed. However, it’s still important to be mindful of potential irritants. Avoid tight clothing that rubs against the piercing and be careful when applying makeup or lotions nearby. Regular inspection of the piercing site can help you catch any potential problems early on. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q: How long does it take for a dermal piercing to heal?**

A: Dermal piercings typically take 6-12 months to fully heal, sometimes longer depending on individual factors and placement. Patience is key during this process.

**Q: Can I shower with a new dermal piercing?**

A: Yes, you can shower, but avoid directing the showerhead directly at the piercing. Gentle rinsing is fine.

**Q: What if my dermal piercing is rejecting?**

A: Rejection occurs when the body pushes the jewelry out. Signs include redness, swelling, and the jewelry moving noticeably. Consult your piercer immediately.

**Q: Is it normal for my dermal piercing to have a little clear fluid?**

A: Yes, a small amount of clear fluid (lymph) is normal during the initial healing phase. However, if the fluid is yellow or green, it could indicate an infection.

**Q: Can I change the jewelry in my dermal piercing myself?**

A: It’s generally not recommended to change the jewelry yourself until the piercing is fully healed. A professional piercer can ensure the new jewelry is properly inserted and minimize the risk of complications.
