## Sparkling Clear: How To Clean Storm Windows

Are your storm windows looking a little…stormy? Grime, dirt, and pollen can quickly build up, obscuring your view and diminishing the light in your home. Cleaning storm windows doesn’t have to be a daunting task. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to get your storm windows sparkling clean, from gathering your supplies to choosing the right cleaning solutions. We’ll cover different types of storm windows and offer tips for tackling tough grime.

**Takeaway:**

*   Gather your supplies: bucket, cleaning solution, cloths, squeegee.
*   Prepare the area: protect floors and surrounding surfaces.
*   Clean the frames and tracks first, then the glass.
*   Dry thoroughly to prevent streaks.
*   Regular cleaning prevents buildup and makes future cleaning easier.

**Quick Answer:** To clean storm windows, mix a solution of warm water and dish soap, apply it to the glass with a cloth, scrub gently, and use a squeegee for a streak-free finish. Don’t forget to clean the frames and tracks first!

## 1. Gathering Your Storm Window Cleaning Supplies

Before you begin, having the right tools makes the job much easier. You won’t need anything fancy, just a few essential items. Start with a bucket to hold your cleaning solution. A good quality microfiber cloth is essential for wiping down surfaces and preventing streaks. You’ll also need a squeegee for efficiently cleaning the glass. Finally, consider a soft brush or old toothbrush for getting into those hard-to-reach tracks and corners. 

For the cleaning solution, you can use a simple mix of warm water and a few drops of dish soap. Alternatively, a commercial glass cleaner works well too. If you're dealing with stubborn grime, a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water can be very effective.  For more information on keeping your windows clean, check out this guide on [how often to clean windows](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-often-to-clean-windows).

## 2. Preparing the Area for Cleaning

Protecting your surroundings is a crucial step. Cleaning solutions can drip, and you don’t want to damage your floors or furniture. Lay down drop cloths or old towels around the base of the windows. This will catch any drips and prevent staining. 

Open the windows slightly to allow for ventilation. This is especially important if you’re using a commercial glass cleaner.  Remove any curtains, blinds, or other window coverings to give you clear access to the glass.  Finally, take a moment to clear the windowsill of any clutter. A clean workspace makes the entire process smoother and more efficient.

## 3. Cleaning Storm Window Frames and Tracks

Don’t skip this step! The frames and tracks often accumulate dust, dirt, and even insect debris. Start by using a vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment to remove loose debris from the tracks. Then, mix a solution of warm water and dish soap, and use a soft brush or toothbrush to scrub the tracks thoroughly. 

For the frames, wipe them down with the same soapy water solution. Pay attention to corners and crevices where dirt tends to accumulate. If the frames are painted, be gentle to avoid damaging the paint.  If you have aluminum storm windows, you might find this article helpful: [how to clean aluminum storm windows](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-aluminum-storm-windows).

## 4. Cleaning the Storm Window Glass: The Squeegee Technique

Now for the main event: cleaning the glass! Dip your microfiber cloth into your cleaning solution and wipe down the entire glass surface. This will loosen any remaining dirt and grime. Next, it’s time to use the squeegee. 

Start at the top corner of the window and overlap each stroke slightly. Wipe the squeegee blade clean with a cloth after each stroke to prevent streaks.  For larger windows, work in sections.  If you’re new to using a squeegee, don’t worry if it takes a little practice to get a streak-free finish.  You can also find helpful tips on cleaning windows [through screens](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-windows-through-screens) if you haven't removed them.

## 5. Dealing with Stubborn Grime and Residue

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, stubborn grime remains. For tough spots, try using a paste of baking soda and water. Apply the paste to the grime, let it sit for a few minutes, and then gently scrub with a soft cloth. 

If you’re dealing with hard water stains, a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water can work wonders. Spray the solution onto the stains, let it sit for a few minutes, and then wipe clean.  For particularly challenging residue, consider a specialized glass cleaner designed for removing tough stains.  If your windows are particularly dirty, you might want to read about [how to clean dirty windows](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-dirty-windows).

## 6. Drying and Finishing Touches for Spotless Storm Windows

Drying is just as important as cleaning! Use a clean, dry microfiber cloth to wipe down the glass and frames. This will prevent water spots and streaks. Pay attention to the edges and corners, ensuring they are completely dry. 

Inspect the windows for any remaining streaks or smudges. If you find any, simply re-wet your cloth with clean water and wipe them away. Once the windows are completely dry, you can replace your curtains or blinds and enjoy your sparkling clean view.

## 7. Maintaining Clean Storm Windows: Prevention is Key

Regular cleaning is the best way to prevent grime buildup and keep your storm windows looking their best. Aim to clean your storm windows at least twice a year – once in the spring and once in the fall. 

Wipe down the frames and tracks weekly to prevent dust and dirt from accumulating. Consider using a window cleaning spray as part of your regular cleaning routine. By taking a few simple steps, you can keep your storm windows sparkling clean and enjoy a brighter, more inviting home.  You can also learn about [how to keep windows clean longer](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-keep-windows-clean-longer) with preventative measures.



## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I use vinegar on all types of storm windows?**
A: Yes, vinegar is generally safe for most storm window types, but always test it on a small, inconspicuous area first, especially if you have painted frames. Vinegar is a natural cleaner and effective for removing hard water stains and grime.

**Q: What’s the best way to clean storm windows that haven’t been cleaned in years?**
A: Start by soaking the windows with warm, soapy water to loosen the grime. Then, use a soft brush to scrub away stubborn dirt. You may need to repeat this process several times. A vinegar solution can also help with tough stains.

**Q: How do I clean storm windows that are difficult to reach?**
A: Invest in a window cleaning kit with an extension pole. This will allow you to reach high windows safely and efficiently. Alternatively, you can hire a professional window cleaning service.

**Q: Are there any cleaning products I should avoid using on storm windows?**
A: Avoid using abrasive cleaners, as they can scratch the glass. Also, avoid using ammonia-based cleaners on tinted windows, as they can damage the tint.

Cleaning storm windows doesn’t have to be a chore. By following these simple steps, you can achieve sparkling clean results and enjoy a brighter, more inviting home. Remember to gather your supplies, prepare the area, clean the frames and tracks, and dry thoroughly. Regular maintenance will prevent grime buildup and keep your storm windows looking their best. So, grab your cleaning supplies and get ready to enjoy a clear view! Don't forget, a little effort goes a long way in maintaining the beauty and functionality of your home.
