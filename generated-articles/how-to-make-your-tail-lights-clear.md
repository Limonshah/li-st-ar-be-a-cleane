## Restore Visibility: How To Make Your Tail Lights Clear

Have you noticed your tail lights looking hazy, yellowed, or just generally dim? This is a common problem, but thankfully, it’s one you can often fix yourself. Clear tail lights aren’t just about aesthetics; they’re crucial for safety, ensuring other drivers can see you, especially at night or in bad weather. This guide will walk you through several methods to make your tail lights clear again, from simple cleaning to more involved restoration techniques. We’ll cover everything you need to know to get your lights shining brightly and keep you safe on the road.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents buildup.
*   Polishing removes oxidation and scratches.
*   UV protection maintains clarity long-term.
*   Proper tools are key for effective restoration.

**Quick Answer:** To make your tail lights clear, start by washing them with soap and water. Then, use a plastic polish or a dedicated tail light restoration kit to remove oxidation and scratches. Finally, protect the restored lights with a UV sealant to prevent future damage.

## Understanding Why Tail Lights Become Cloudy

Before diving into the “how-to,” let’s understand *why* tail lights lose their clarity. The primary culprit is oxidation, a chemical reaction caused by exposure to sunlight, heat, and environmental pollutants. The plastic used in tail lights (typically polycarbonate) gradually breaks down over time. This breakdown creates a cloudy or yellowed appearance.  Small scratches and abrasions on the surface also contribute to the hazy look, trapping dirt and grime. Ignoring this issue doesn’t make it go away; it worsens, reducing visibility and potentially leading to safety concerns.

## Simple Cleaning: The First Step to Clear Tail Lights

Often, what appears to be deep cloudiness is simply accumulated dirt and grime. Start with the easiest solution: a thorough cleaning.  You’ll need mild soap (like car wash soap), water, a soft sponge or microfiber cloth, and a garden hose.  Wash the tail lights just as you would the rest of your car, paying attention to any stubborn dirt. Rinse thoroughly and dry with a clean microfiber cloth.  This simple step can often significantly improve the appearance of your tail lights. If you want to take it a step further, consider using a dedicated plastic cleaner, like the one used for [https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-clear-plastic](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-clear-plastic), to remove any remaining residue.

## Polishing Away Oxidation: Restoring Clarity

If cleaning doesn’t fully restore clarity, polishing is your next best bet. Polishing removes a thin layer of the oxidized plastic, revealing the clear material underneath. You have a couple of options here: a dedicated tail light restoration kit or individual polishing compounds.  Kits typically include sandpaper (various grits), polishing compounds, and UV protectant. If you choose individual compounds, you’ll need a plastic polish specifically designed for removing oxidation. 

Here’s a basic polishing process:

1.  **Sand:** Start with a coarser grit sandpaper (e.g., 800 grit) to remove significant oxidation. Gradually move to finer grits (e.g., 1500, 2000, 3000) to smooth the surface.
2.  **Polish:** Apply a plastic polish to a polishing pad attached to a drill or orbital polisher. Work the polish over the tail light in circular motions.
3.  **Repeat:** Repeat the polishing process with finer polishing compounds for a clearer finish.
4.  **Clean:** Remove any polishing residue with a clean microfiber cloth.

## The Power of UV Protection: Preventing Future Damage

Polishing restores clarity, but it doesn’t prevent oxidation from happening again.  UV rays are the primary cause of the problem, so protecting your tail lights with a UV sealant is crucial.  Many tail light restoration kits include a UV protectant, but you can also purchase it separately. Apply the sealant according to the product instructions, ensuring a thin, even coat. This protective layer will shield the plastic from the sun’s harmful rays, keeping your tail lights clear for longer.  Think of it like sunscreen for your car’s lights! ## Maintaining Clear Tail Lights: Preventative Measures

Once you’ve restored your tail lights, you’ll want to keep them that way. Regular cleaning is the most important preventative measure. Wash your car frequently, paying attention to the tail lights.  Consider applying a coat of wax or sealant to the tail lights every few months for added protection.  Parking in the shade whenever possible can also help minimize UV exposure.  By taking these simple steps, you can significantly extend the life of your tail lights and maintain their clarity for years to come. ## FAQ: Your Tail Light Questions Answered

**Q: How often should I polish my tail lights?**

A:  It depends on your climate and how much sun exposure your car gets. Generally, polishing every 6-12 months is sufficient if you’re also using a UV protectant.

**Q: Can I use toothpaste to clean my tail lights?**

A: While toothpaste *can* act as a mild polishing agent, it’s not as effective as dedicated plastic polishes and may leave residue.

**Q: Will restoring my tail lights pass inspection?**

A:  In most cases, yes. Many states require functional and visible tail lights for vehicle inspections. Restoring clarity ensures your lights meet these requirements.

**Q: What if the plastic is cracked?**

A:  Cracked tail lights require replacement. Polishing won’t fix structural damage.
