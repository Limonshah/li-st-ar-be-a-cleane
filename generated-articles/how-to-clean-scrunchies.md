## Keeping Your Scrunchies Fresh: A Complete Cleaning Guide

Do you love scrunchies? These gentle hair ties are back in style, and for good reason! They’re kinder to your hair than traditional elastics, but they *do* collect oil, dust, and hairspray residue. Learning [how to clean scrunchies](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-scrunchies) is essential for keeping them in good condition and preventing buildup that can damage your hair. This article will walk you through everything you need to know, from hand washing to machine washing, and even how to deal with different scrunchie fabrics.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents buildup and extends the life of your scrunchies.
*   Hand washing is the gentlest method, especially for delicate fabrics.
*   Machine washing is convenient, but requires a laundry bag and gentle cycle.
*   Proper drying prevents stretching and damage.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean scrunchies, hand wash them in cool water with a mild detergent, gently scrub, rinse thoroughly, and air dry. For machine washing, place them in a mesh laundry bag and use a delicate cycle.

## Why Cleaning Your Scrunchies Matters

It’s easy to forget about cleaning your scrunchies, but they really need it! Think about everything they come into contact with – hair products, natural oils from your scalp, and even dust from the air. This buildup can make your scrunchies stiff, lose their elasticity, and even transfer grime back into your clean hair. Regularly cleaning your scrunchies keeps them soft, stretchy, and hygienic. Plus, a little care goes a long way in extending their lifespan, saving you money in the long run.

## Hand Washing: The Gentle Approach

Hand washing is the safest and most recommended method for cleaning scrunchies, especially those made from delicate materials like silk or velvet. It’s a simple process that ensures your scrunchies won’t get damaged. Here’s how to do it:

1.  **Fill a basin:** Fill a clean sink or basin with cool water. Avoid hot water, as it can damage some fabrics.
2.  **Add detergent:** Add a small amount of mild detergent – think baby shampoo or a delicate fabric wash.
3.  **Soak and swirl:** Place your scrunchies in the soapy water and gently swirl them around. Let them soak for 10-15 minutes.
4.  **Gentle scrub:** If needed, gently scrub any visible dirt or buildup with your fingers.
5.  **Rinse thoroughly:** Rinse the scrunchies under cool, running water until all the soap is gone.
6.  **Air dry:** Gently squeeze out excess water (don’t wring!) and lay them flat to air dry.

## Machine Washing: Convenience with Caution

While hand washing is ideal, machine washing can be a convenient option if you have a lot of scrunchies to clean. However, it’s important to take precautions to protect them. You can also consider [how to clean a backpack](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-backpack) using the same gentle approach.

*   **Use a laundry bag:** Always place your scrunchies inside a mesh laundry bag to prevent them from getting tangled or snagged in the washing machine.
*   **Delicate cycle:** Wash them on a delicate cycle with cool water.
*   **Mild detergent:** Use a mild detergent, just like you would for hand washing.
*   **Avoid harsh chemicals:** Don’t use bleach or fabric softener, as these can damage the fabric.
*   **Skip the spin cycle:** If possible, skip the spin cycle or use the lowest spin setting to minimize stretching.

## Dealing with Different Scrunchie Fabrics

Different fabrics require slightly different care. Here’s a breakdown of how to clean common scrunchie materials:

*   **Cotton:** Cotton scrunchies are generally durable and can withstand machine washing. Follow the machine washing instructions above.
*   **Silk:** Silk scrunchies are delicate and should always be hand washed. Avoid harsh detergents and direct sunlight when drying.
*   **Velvet:** Velvet scrunchies are also delicate and best hand washed. Be extra gentle when handling them to avoid crushing the pile.
*   **Satin:** Similar to silk, satin scrunchies benefit from hand washing with a mild detergent.
*   **Polyester/Spandex:** These synthetic fabrics are relatively durable and can be machine washed, but still benefit from a laundry bag and gentle cycle.

## Drying Your Scrunchies Properly

Proper drying is just as important as washing. Avoid using a dryer, as the heat can cause the elastic to lose its stretch and the fabric to shrink or become damaged. Instead, lay your scrunchies flat on a clean towel or hang them to air dry. Make sure they are completely dry before storing them to prevent mildew. ## Preventing Buildup and Extending Scrunchie Life

Beyond regular cleaning, there are a few things you can do to prevent buildup and keep your scrunchies in top condition:

*   **Don’t share:** Avoid sharing your scrunchies with others to prevent the spread of oils and bacteria.
*   **Use clean hands:** Try to use clean hands when putting your hair up with a scrunchie.
*   **Rotate your scrunchies:** Don’t wear the same scrunchie every day. Rotating them allows them to air out and recover their shape.
*   **Store properly:** Store your scrunchies in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.



## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I clean my scrunchies?**

A: It depends on how often you use them, but generally, cleaning your scrunchies every 1-2 weeks is a good idea. If you use a lot of hair products, you may need to clean them more frequently.

**Q: Can I use bleach on my scrunchies?**

A: No, absolutely not! Bleach can damage the fabric and cause discoloration. Always use a mild detergent.

**Q: My scrunchie has a stain. How can I remove it?**

A: For minor stains, try spot cleaning with a damp cloth and a small amount of mild detergent. For stubborn stains, you may need to consult a professional cleaner.

**Q: Will washing my scrunchies make the elastic lose its stretch?**

A: Washing can contribute to elastic breakdown over time, but using cool water, a gentle cycle, and avoiding the dryer will help minimize this.

**Q: Can I iron my scrunchies?**

A: It’s generally not recommended to iron scrunchies, especially those made from delicate fabrics. If you must iron, use the lowest setting and place a thin cloth between the iron and the scrunchie.
