## Restore Suction: How To Clean Your Dyson Vacuum Head Effectively

Dyson vacuums are powerful tools for keeping our homes clean. However, even the best vacuums need regular care to maintain their strong performance. The vacuum head, also known as the cleaner head or brush bar assembly, works hard, picking up dirt, dust, and hair from your floors. Over time, this constant work causes debris to build up, especially on the brush bar. This buildup can significantly reduce suction power and cleaning efficiency. I often notice my Dyson struggling more than usual when the head is dirty.

This article provides a complete guide on how to clean your Dyson vacuum head. We will cover why cleaning the head is important and what tools you need. You will learn how to safely disassemble your Dyson cleaner head, remove tangled hair, and clean all components. We will also share tips for reassembly and routine maintenance. Follow these steps to ensure your Dyson vacuum continues to perform at its best, making your cleaning tasks easier.

### Takeaway

*   Regularly clean your Dyson vacuum head to maintain strong suction and effective cleaning.
*   Gather simple tools like scissors, a coin, and a cloth before you start.
*   Safely disconnect power and disassemble the cleaner head components.
*   Thoroughly remove tangled hair and debris from the brush bar.
*   Clean the main housing and check for blockages.
*   Allow all parts to dry completely before reassembling.
*   Perform routine checks to prevent future buildup.

### Clear Answer

To clean a Dyson vacuum head, first disconnect the vacuum from power. Use a coin to unlock the end cap and remove the brush bar. Cut and remove all tangled hair and debris from the brush bar. Wipe down the brush bar and the inside of the cleaner head. Reassemble once all parts are dry.
