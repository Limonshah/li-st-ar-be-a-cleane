## Keeping Your Pontoon Sparkling: How To Clean Pontoon Boat Sides

Is your pontoon boat looking a little worse for wear? Don't worry, restoring its shine is easier than you think! Cleaning pontoon boat sides regularly not only improves its appearance but also protects the aluminum from damage. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to effectively clean your pontoon boat sides, from choosing the right cleaning products to employing the best techniques. We’ll cover everything from basic washing to tackling stubborn stains, ensuring your boat looks its best all season long. Let's dive in and get your pontoon looking pristine!

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents buildup and makes future cleaning easier.
*   Use mild soap and water for routine washes.
*   Address stains promptly to avoid permanent discoloration.
*   Protect your aluminum with appropriate cleaning products and techniques.

**Quick Answer:** To clean pontoon boat sides, wash with mild soap and water, scrub gently with a soft brush, rinse thoroughly, and apply a protective wax. Address stains with specialized cleaners designed for aluminum.

## 1. Gathering Your Cleaning Supplies for Pontoon Boat Sides

Before you start, having the right tools makes the job much easier. You won’t need anything too fancy, just a few essentials. First, grab a bucket and fill it with warm water and a mild boat soap. Avoid harsh detergents or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the aluminum. You’ll also need a soft-bristled brush – a car wash brush works well – and several clean microfiber cloths. A garden hose with a spray nozzle is essential for rinsing. For tougher stains, consider a dedicated aluminum boat cleaner. Finally, having a wax specifically designed for aluminum boats will help protect the sides after cleaning. [Learn more about cleaning your entire boat here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-boat).

## 2. The Basic Wash: Routine Cleaning for Pontoon Sides

Regular washing is the key to keeping your pontoon boat sides looking their best. Start by rinsing the sides with your garden hose to remove loose dirt and debris. Then, dip your soft-bristled brush into your soapy water and gently scrub the sides, working in sections. Pay attention to areas that accumulate dirt, like around railings and seating areas. Avoid applying excessive pressure, as this could scratch the aluminum. Once you’ve scrubbed the entire surface, rinse thoroughly with clean water, ensuring all soap residue is removed. This routine wash should be done after each use, or at least every few weeks, to prevent buildup.

## 3. Tackling Stubborn Stains on Your Pontoon

Sometimes, a simple wash isn’t enough. Stubborn stains like water spots, fish scales, or algae require a bit more attention. For water spots, try a solution of white vinegar and water. Apply it to the affected area, let it sit for a few minutes, then scrub gently and rinse. For tougher stains, use an aluminum boat cleaner specifically designed to remove them. Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully. Remember to test any cleaner in an inconspicuous area first to ensure it doesn’t damage the aluminum. If you're dealing with mold or mildew, a specialized mold remover might be necessary. [You can find more information on cleaning boat cushions here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-boat-cushions).

## 4. Cleaning Aluminum Rails Alongside Pontoon Sides

Often, the sides of your pontoon are connected to aluminum rails. These require similar care, but sometimes need extra attention. Use the same mild soap and water solution, but pay close attention to any corrosion that might be forming. For light corrosion, a mixture of baking soda and water can be effective. Apply it to the corroded areas, let it sit, then scrub with a soft brush. For more severe corrosion, you may need a specialized aluminum polish. Remember to rinse thoroughly after using any cleaning product. Keeping your aluminum rails clean and protected will extend their lifespan and maintain the overall appearance of your pontoon. [For a deeper dive into cleaning aluminum rails, check out this guide](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-aluminum-rails-on-a-pontoon-boat).

## 5. Protecting Your Pontoon Sides with Wax

After cleaning, protecting your pontoon boat sides is crucial. Applying a wax specifically designed for aluminum boats creates a barrier against the elements, preventing corrosion and making future cleaning easier. Apply the wax according to the manufacturer’s instructions, typically using a clean microfiber cloth. Work in small sections, applying a thin, even coat. Allow the wax to dry, then buff it to a shine with a clean cloth. Waxing your pontoon sides a few times a year will significantly extend their lifespan and keep them looking their best.

## 6. Dealing with Lake Scum and Waterlines

Lake scum and waterlines are common problems for pontoon boats. These unsightly marks are caused by algae, minerals, and other debris that accumulate along the waterline. To remove them, use a dedicated waterline cleaner or a mixture of baking soda and water. Apply the cleaner to the affected area, let it sit for a few minutes, then scrub gently with a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly with clean water. For stubborn waterlines, you may need to repeat the process several times. Preventing waterline buildup is easier than removing it, so consider using a boat protectant spray to create a barrier. [If you're struggling with lake scum specifically, this article can help](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-lake-scum-off-the-aluminum-boat).

## 7. Preventing Future Dirt and Grime Buildup

Once you’ve cleaned your pontoon boat sides, taking steps to prevent future dirt and grime buildup is essential. After each use, rinse the sides with fresh water to remove any salt, dirt, or debris. Cover your boat when it’s not in use to protect it from the elements. Regularly inspect the sides for any signs of corrosion or staining and address them promptly. By following these simple preventative measures, you can keep your pontoon boat sides looking their best for years to come.



## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What’s the best way to remove bird droppings from pontoon boat sides?**
A: Bird droppings can be acidic and damaging. Remove them as quickly as possible with warm, soapy water and a soft cloth. If the stain persists, use a dedicated bird dropping remover designed for aluminum.

**Q: Can I use a pressure washer to clean my pontoon boat sides?**
A: While a pressure washer can be tempting, it’s generally not recommended. The high pressure can damage the aluminum and potentially strip the paint. If you must use one, use a very low setting and a wide nozzle.

**Q: How often should I wax my pontoon boat sides?**
A: Waxing your pontoon boat sides 2-3 times per year is ideal, especially if you use your boat frequently or live in a harsh climate.

**Q: What type of soap is safe to use on pontoon boat sides?**
A: Always use a mild boat soap specifically formulated for marine use. Avoid harsh detergents, abrasive cleaners, and household cleaning products.

**Q: Is it okay to use steel wool to clean aluminum pontoon sides?**
A: Absolutely not! Steel wool will scratch and damage the aluminum surface. Always use a soft-bristled brush or microfiber cloth.
