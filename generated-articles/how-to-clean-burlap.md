## How to Clean Burlap: A Complete Guide

Have you fallen in love with the rustic charm of burlap? It’s a wonderfully versatile fabric, perfect for everything from home décor to crafting. But keeping burlap looking its best can be tricky. This guide will show you exactly [how to clean burlap](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-burlap) safely and effectively, preserving its natural beauty for years to come. We’ll cover everything from quick spot treatments to more thorough cleaning methods. Let’s dive in and learn how to care for this unique material!

**Takeaway:**

*   Avoid harsh chemicals and excessive water.
*   Spot clean stains immediately for best results.
*   Hand washing is generally safer than machine washing.
*   Air dry burlap completely to prevent mildew.

**Can you wash burlap?**

Yes, you can wash burlap, but it requires a gentle approach. Burlap is a natural fiber and can shrink or become damaged with harsh treatment. Hand washing is the preferred method, but some items can be machine washed on a delicate cycle.

## Understanding Burlap: What You Need to Know

Burlap, also known as hessian, is a coarse, woven fabric made from the jute plant. It’s known for its durability and affordability, but it’s also quite porous. This means it absorbs liquids easily, making it prone to staining and potential mildew. Understanding this characteristic is key to successful burlap cleaning. Because of its natural composition, burlap doesn’t respond well to strong detergents or aggressive scrubbing. 

It’s important to test any cleaning solution on a hidden area of the burlap first to ensure it doesn’t cause discoloration or damage. Knowing the origin of your burlap can also help; some burlap is treated with sizing or finishes that may affect cleaning. ## Spot Cleaning Burlap: Quick Stain Removal

Immediate action is crucial when it comes to burlap stains. The porous nature of the fabric means spills can set quickly. Start by gently blotting the stain with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the stain and damage the fibers. Next, create a mild cleaning solution by mixing a small amount of gentle dish soap with cool water. 

Dampen a clean cloth with the solution and gently blot the stain, working from the outside in. Once the stain is lifted, rinse the area with a clean, damp cloth to remove any soap residue. Finally, allow the burlap to air dry completely. For stubborn stains, you might try a paste of baking soda and water, applied gently and rinsed thoroughly.

## Hand Washing Burlap: A Gentle Approach

For a more thorough cleaning, hand washing is the safest option. Fill a clean basin or tub with cool water and add a small amount of mild detergent specifically designed for delicate fabrics. Submerge the burlap item and gently agitate the water to loosen dirt and grime. Avoid harsh scrubbing or twisting, as this can distort the weave.

Let the burlap soak for about 15-20 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with cool, clean water until all the soap is removed. Gently squeeze out excess water – do *not* wring the burlap. Lay the item flat on a clean, dry towel and roll it up to absorb more moisture. ## Machine Washing Burlap: Proceed with Caution

While hand washing is preferred, some burlap items can be machine washed. However, it’s essential to take precautions. Place the burlap item in a mesh laundry bag to protect it from snagging or tearing. Use a delicate cycle with cold water and a mild detergent. 

Avoid using bleach or fabric softener, as these can damage the fibers. After washing, gently remove the burlap from the bag and lay it flat to dry.  Machine washing is riskier, so always test a small, inconspicuous area first.

## Drying Burlap: Air Drying is Key

Regardless of how you wash your burlap, air drying is the best method. Avoid using a dryer, as the heat can cause the fabric to shrink and become brittle. Lay the burlap item flat on a clean, dry towel or hang it on a clothesline in a well-ventilated area. 

Ensure the burlap is completely dry before storing it to prevent mildew growth.  Turning the item over periodically will help it dry evenly.  If you're dealing with mildew, address it promptly to prevent further damage. ## Protecting Burlap: Preventing Future Stains

Prevention is always better than cure! Consider applying a fabric protector spray specifically designed for natural fibers to help repel stains and water. This can be especially helpful for items that are frequently used or exposed to spills. Regularly vacuuming or brushing burlap items can also help remove dust and dirt, preventing them from becoming embedded in the fibers. 

Store burlap items in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and mildew.  By taking these preventative measures, you can keep your burlap looking beautiful for years to come.



## Burlap Cleaning: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I iron burlap?**

A: Ironing burlap is generally not recommended, as the heat can damage the fibers. If you must iron, use a low heat setting and place a clean cloth between the iron and the burlap.

**Q: How do I remove mildew from burlap?**

A: Mix equal parts white vinegar and water, and gently apply to the mildewed area. Let it sit for 15-20 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with clean water and allow to air dry.

**Q: Is burlap colorfast?**

A: Burlap can sometimes bleed color, especially during the first wash. It’s always best to test a hidden area first and wash with similar colors.

**Q: Can I use bleach on burlap?**

A: No, bleach should *never* be used on burlap, as it can severely damage the fibers and cause discoloration.

**Q: What's the best way to clean burlap table runners?**

A: Spot clean spills immediately. For a deeper clean, hand wash gently with cool water and mild detergent, then air dry flat.
