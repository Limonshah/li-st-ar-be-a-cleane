## Keeping Your Floors Fresh: How To Clean a Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro Brush

Is your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro losing suction or leaving streaks on your floors? Often, the culprit is a dirty brush roll. Pet hair, dust, and debris can quickly build up, hindering its cleaning power. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to thoroughly clean your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro brush, restoring its performance and keeping your floors sparkling. We’ll cover everything from quick daily maintenance to deep cleaning steps.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regularly remove hair and debris from the brush roll after each use.
*   Deep clean the brush roll at least once a month, or more often with heavy pet shedding.
*   Disassemble and clean the brush roll components for a truly thorough clean.
*   Ensure all parts are completely dry before reassembling.

**Quick Answer:**

To clean your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro brush, remove it from the machine, clear visible debris, use scissors to cut away wrapped hair, and wash with warm, soapy water. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely before reinstalling.

## Why Cleaning Your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro Brush Matters

A clean brush roll is essential for optimal cleaning performance. When the brush is clogged with hair and debris, it can’t effectively agitate and lift dirt from your floors. This leads to reduced suction, streaking, and an overall less satisfying clean. Plus, a buildup of grime can damage the brush motor over time, shortening the lifespan of your machine. Taking the time to regularly clean your brush roll will save you time, effort, and money in the long run. It’s a simple maintenance task that makes a big difference.

## Daily Maintenance: Quick Hair Removal

After each use of your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro, take a moment to remove any visible hair or debris from the brush roll. This quick step prevents buildup and keeps your machine performing at its best. You can use your hands, but a small tool like scissors or a seam ripper works even better. Carefully cut away any hair that’s wrapped around the brush bristles. Don’t pull, as this can damage the bristles. This simple habit will significantly reduce the need for deep cleaning. ## Deep Cleaning Your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro Brush (Monthly)

Once a month, or more frequently if you have shedding pets, give your brush roll a more thorough cleaning. Start by removing the brush roll from the machine – the owner’s manual will have specific instructions for your model. Next, prepare a sink or basin with warm water and a mild dish soap. Submerge the brush roll and gently scrub it with your hands or a soft brush. Pay attention to the areas where hair and debris tend to accumulate. Rinse the brush roll thoroughly under clean water until all soap residue is gone.

### Removing Stubborn Debris

Sometimes, hair and debris can become deeply embedded in the brush roll. For these stubborn messes, try using an old toothbrush or a small cleaning brush to dislodge the grime. You can also use a pet hair removal tool, like a rubber brush or a lint roller, to help lift away stubborn strands. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the brush bristles. Remember, gentle but persistent cleaning is key.

## Disassembling and Cleaning Brush Roll Components

For the most thorough clean, disassemble the brush roll. Many Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro brush rolls can be taken apart to access the inner core and bristles. Consult your owner’s manual for specific disassembly instructions. Once disassembled, wash each component separately with warm, soapy water. Use a small brush to clean between the bristles and remove any hidden debris. Rinse thoroughly and allow all parts to air dry completely before reassembling. This level of cleaning ensures every part is free of buildup.

## Drying and Reassembling the Brush Roll

Proper drying is crucial to prevent mold and mildew growth. After washing all the brush roll components, lay them out on a clean towel and allow them to air dry completely. This may take several hours, or even overnight. Ensure all parts are completely dry before reassembling the brush roll. Once dry, carefully reassemble the brush roll according to your owner’s manual. Reinstall the brush roll into your Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro and you’re ready to go! ## Troubleshooting: Brush Roll Not Spinning?

If your brush roll isn’t spinning after cleaning, there are a few things you can check. First, ensure the brush roll is properly installed and locked into place. Second, check for any obstructions that may be preventing the brush from rotating. Finally, if the problem persists, there may be an issue with the brush motor. In this case, it’s best to contact Bissell customer support for assistance. Sometimes, a simple reset of the machine can also resolve the issue. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I clean my Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro brush?**

A: We recommend a quick hair removal after each use and a deep clean at least once a month. If you have shedding pets, you may need to deep clean more frequently.

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my Bissell Crosswave Pet Pro brush?**

A: No, avoid using bleach or harsh chemicals, as they can damage the brush bristles and other components. Stick to warm water and mild dish soap.

**Q: How long does it take for the brush roll to dry completely?**

A: It can take several hours, or even overnight, for the brush roll and its components to dry completely. Ensure everything is thoroughly dry before reassembling.

**Q: What if my brush roll is still dirty after cleaning?**

A: Try disassembling the brush roll and cleaning each component separately. You can also use a pet hair removal tool to help lift away stubborn debris.

**Q: Can I put the brush roll in the dishwasher?**

A: No, it is not recommended to put the brush roll in the dishwasher. The high heat and harsh detergents can damage the bristles and other components.
