## Keeping Your Braids Fresh: How to Clean Braiding Hair

Have you ever wondered how to properly care for your braiding hair to keep it looking its best? Braiding hair, whether synthetic or human, requires regular cleaning to remove buildup, prevent odors, and extend its lifespan. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about cleaning your braiding hair, from preparation to maintenance. We’ll cover different hair types and provide simple, effective methods to keep your braids looking fabulous.

**Takeaway:**

*   Clean braiding hair *before* installation to remove manufacturer residue.
*   Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner formulated for synthetic or human hair.
*   Rinse thoroughly to avoid product buildup.
*   Allow hair to air dry completely before braiding.
*   Regularly cleanse your scalp and braids to maintain hygiene.

**Quick Answer:** To clean braiding hair, gently wash it with a mild shampoo in cool water, rinse thoroughly, apply conditioner, and let it air dry completely before installing or maintaining your braids. This removes debris and product buildup, keeping your braids fresh and healthy.

## Why Cleaning Braiding Hair Matters

Before diving into the *how*, let’s discuss the *why*. New braiding hair often has a coating from the manufacturing process. This coating can cause itching, flaking, and even a dull appearance. Cleaning removes this residue, preparing the hair for a smooth and comfortable installation. Plus, regular cleaning during wear prevents buildup of oils, dirt, and styling products, which can lead to odors and damage the hair fibers. 

Think of it like this: you wouldn’t wear clothes without washing them first, right? The same principle applies to braiding hair. Keeping it clean is essential for both hygiene and aesthetics. ## Cleaning Braiding Hair Before Installation

Cleaning braiding hair *before* you braid it is a crucial step often overlooked. This initial cleanse removes the factory coating and prepares the hair for a longer-lasting, more comfortable style. Here’s how to do it:

1.  **Detangle:** Gently detangle the hair with a wide-tooth comb, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
2.  **Wash:** Fill a basin or sink with cool water and add a small amount of mild shampoo. Gently submerge the hair and swish it around, avoiding harsh rubbing.
3.  **Rinse:** Rinse the hair thoroughly with cool water until all the shampoo is removed.
4.  **Condition:** Apply a lightweight conditioner to the hair, focusing on the ends. Let it sit for a few minutes, then rinse again.
5.  **Dry:** Gently squeeze out excess water and lay the hair flat to air dry completely. Avoid using heat, as this can damage the fibers.

## How to Clean Synthetic Braiding Hair

Synthetic braiding hair requires a slightly different approach than human hair. It's more prone to tangling and can be damaged by harsh chemicals. Here’s a guide to cleaning synthetic braids:

*   **Use a Gentle Shampoo:** Choose a shampoo specifically designed for synthetic hair or a very mild baby shampoo. Harsh detergents can strip the hair of its moisture and cause it to become brittle.
*   **Avoid Hot Water:** Hot water can melt or distort synthetic fibers. Always use cool or lukewarm water.
*   **Detangle Carefully:** Synthetic hair tangles easily. Use a wide-tooth comb and work slowly and gently to avoid breakage.
*   **Skip the Conditioner (Sometimes):** Some synthetic hair doesn't benefit from conditioner, as it can cause buildup. Check the manufacturer's instructions. If you do use conditioner, use a very small amount and rinse thoroughly.
*   **Air Dry Only:** Never use a hairdryer on synthetic hair. Let it air dry completely.

## Cleaning Human Hair Braiding Hair

Human hair braiding hair can be treated more like your natural hair, but still requires gentle care. Here’s how to clean it:

*   **Sulfate-Free Shampoo:** Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping the hair of its natural oils.
*   **Deep Conditioning:** Human hair benefits from deep conditioning treatments to maintain its moisture and elasticity.
*   **Gentle Handling:** Handle the hair gently to avoid breakage. Avoid harsh rubbing or pulling.
*   **Proper Drying:** Allow the hair to air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
*   **Storage:** Store human hair braiding hair in a clean, dry place to prevent tangling and damage.

## Maintaining Clean Braids During Wear

Cleaning your braids *while* you’re wearing them is just as important as cleaning them beforehand. This helps to prevent buildup and maintain a healthy scalp.

*   **Scalp Cleansing:** Use a diluted shampoo or a scalp cleanser to gently cleanse your scalp. Focus on the roots of the braids.
*   **Braids Rinse:** After cleansing your scalp, rinse thoroughly to remove all traces of shampoo.
*   **Light Conditioning:** Apply a lightweight leave-in conditioner to the braids to keep them moisturized.
*   **Oil Your Scalp:** Regularly oil your scalp with a natural oil like jojoba or coconut oil to prevent dryness and itching.
*   **Wrap at Night:** Wrap your braids with a satin scarf or bonnet at night to protect them from friction and breakage.

## Dealing with Stubborn Buildup and Odors

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, buildup and odors can still occur. Here’s how to tackle them:

*   **Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse:** Mix equal parts apple cider vinegar and water and soak the braids for 15-20 minutes. This helps to remove buildup and restore the hair’s pH balance.
*   **Baking Soda Paste:** Create a paste of baking soda and water and apply it to the affected areas. Let it sit for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly. *Use sparingly, as baking soda can be drying.*
*   **Tea Tree Oil:** Add a few drops of tea tree oil to your shampoo or conditioner to help combat odors and promote a healthy scalp.
*   **Professional Cleaning:** If you’re struggling to remove stubborn buildup, consider taking your braids to a professional stylist for a deep cleaning treatment.

## FAQ About Cleaning Braiding Hair

**Q: How often should I clean my braiding hair?**
A: It depends on your activity level and how much product you use. Generally, cleaning before installation and then every 2-4 weeks during wear is sufficient.

**Q: Can I use regular shampoo on braiding hair?**
A: It’s best to use a mild, sulfate-free shampoo or one specifically designed for synthetic hair. Regular shampoos can be too harsh and cause damage.

**Q: How do I dry braiding hair quickly?**
A: Avoid using heat. Air drying is the safest option. You can gently blot the hair with a towel to remove excess water.

**Q: What if my braiding hair smells even after cleaning?**
A: Try an apple cider vinegar rinse or add a few drops of tea tree oil to your shampoo. If the odor persists, it may be time to replace the hair.

**Q: Is it okay to use conditioner on synthetic braiding hair?**
A: It depends on the type of synthetic hair. Some synthetic fibers don't benefit from conditioner and can become sticky. Check the manufacturer's instructions.
