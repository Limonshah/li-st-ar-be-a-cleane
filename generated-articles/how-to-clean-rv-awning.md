## How to Clean Your RV Awning: A Step-by-Step Guide

Is your RV awning looking a little drab? Don't let dirt and grime ruin your outdoor enjoyment! Cleaning your RV awning regularly is essential for maintaining its appearance and extending its lifespan. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about how to clean an RV awning, from gathering supplies to preventing future buildup. We’ll cover different awning materials and cleaning methods to ensure you get the best results.

**Takeaway:**

*   Regular cleaning prevents damage and extends awning life.
*   Mild soap and water are usually sufficient for most awnings.
*   Avoid harsh chemicals and abrasive cleaners.
*   Proper drying is crucial to prevent mildew growth.

**Quick Answer:**

Cleaning your RV awning is simple: use mild soap, water, and a soft brush to gently scrub away dirt. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely. Regular cleaning prevents mildew and extends the life of your awning.

## Why Cleaning Your RV Awning Matters

Your RV awning provides valuable shade and shelter during your adventures. However, it’s constantly exposed to the elements – sun, rain, dirt, bird droppings, and tree sap. These can all contribute to unsightly stains and, more importantly, damage the awning fabric over time. A clean awning not only looks better but also performs better, protecting you and your RV. Ignoring regular cleaning can lead to mildew, mold, and even tears in the fabric, requiring costly repairs or replacement.

## Understanding Your RV Awning Material

Before you start cleaning, it’s important to identify your awning’s material. Different materials require different cleaning approaches. Common RV awning materials include:

*   **Vinyl:** Durable and easy to clean, vinyl awnings are the most common type.
*   **Acrylic:** Offers good UV resistance but can be more prone to staining.
*   **Polyester:** Lightweight and affordable, but may require more frequent cleaning.
*   **Canvas:** Less common in newer RVs, canvas awnings require specialized cleaning to avoid damage. If you have a canvas awning, you may want to consult [https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-canvas-awning](https://www.beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-canvas-awning) for specific instructions.

Always check your RV’s owner’s manual for specific cleaning recommendations for your awning.

## Gathering Your Cleaning Supplies

Having the right supplies on hand will make the cleaning process much easier. Here’s what you’ll need:

*   **Mild Dish Soap:** Avoid harsh detergents that can damage the fabric.
*   **Warm Water:** For effective cleaning without being abrasive.
*   **Soft-Bristled Brush:** A long-handled brush is ideal for reaching high areas.
*   **Spray Bottle:** For applying the cleaning solution.
*   **Garden Hose with Spray Nozzle:** For rinsing.
*   **Clean Microfiber Cloths:** For wiping down surfaces.
*   **RV Awning Cleaner (Optional):** Specifically formulated for RV awnings.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your RV Awning

Now that you have your supplies, let’s get cleaning! Follow these steps for a sparkling clean awning:

1.  **Extend the Awning:** Fully extend your RV awning to provide easy access to all areas.
2.  **Prepare the Cleaning Solution:** Mix a small amount of mild dish soap with warm water in a spray bottle.
3.  **Apply the Solution:** Spray the entire awning surface with the cleaning solution.
4.  **Gentle Scrubbing:** Using the soft-bristled brush, gently scrub the awning in a circular motion. Pay extra attention to areas with visible dirt or stains. Avoid applying excessive pressure, as this could damage the fabric.
5.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Use the garden hose with a spray nozzle to rinse the awning completely, removing all traces of soap.
6.  **Inspect and Repeat (If Necessary):** Inspect the awning for any remaining dirt or stains. If needed, repeat steps 3-5.
7.  **Allow to Air Dry:** Allow the awning to air dry completely before retracting it. This is crucial to prevent mildew growth. You can speed up the drying process by using a clean microfiber cloth to wipe down the awning.

## Dealing with Stubborn Stains and Mildew

Sometimes, simple soap and water aren’t enough to tackle stubborn stains or mildew. Here are a few tips:

*   **Bird Droppings:** Soak the affected area with warm water and gently scrub with a soft brush.
*   **Tree Sap:** Use a specialized tree sap remover or a mixture of rubbing alcohol and water. Test in an inconspicuous area first.
*   **Mildew:** For mild mildew, try a solution of water and white vinegar. For more severe mildew, you may need a specialized mildew remover designed for RV awnings. Check out [https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-an-awning-with-mold](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-an-awning-with-mold) for more detailed guidance.
*   **Always test any cleaning solution in a small, hidden area of the awning before applying it to the entire surface.**

## Protecting Your Clean Awning

Once your awning is clean, you’ll want to keep it that way! Here are a few tips for protecting your investment:

*   **Regular Cleaning:** Clean your awning at least twice a year, or more often if you camp in dusty or dirty environments.
*   **Retract During Storms:** Retract your awning during strong winds or heavy rain to prevent damage.
*   **Use an Awning Protector:** Consider applying an awning protector to help repel water and dirt.
*   **Proper Storage:** When storing your RV for extended periods, ensure the awning is completely dry before retracting it. You might also consider using an awning cover. ## FAQ About RV Awning Cleaning

**Q: Can I use a pressure washer to clean my RV awning?**

A: No, a pressure washer can damage the awning fabric. The high pressure can tear or weaken the material. Always use a soft-bristled brush and gentle cleaning solutions.

**Q: What’s the best way to clean a canvas RV awning?**

A: Canvas awnings require specialized cleaning. Use a mild soap specifically designed for canvas and avoid harsh chemicals. Rinse thoroughly and allow to air dry completely.

**Q: How often should I clean my RV awning?**

A: Ideally, you should clean your RV awning at least twice a year. However, if you camp frequently or in dirty environments, you may need to clean it more often.

**Q: Can I use bleach to clean my RV awning?**

A: No, bleach can damage the awning fabric and cause discoloration. Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach.
